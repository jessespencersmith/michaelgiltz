New York Post Archive

Michael Giltz began a prolific relationship with the New York Post -- his introduction to tabloid journalism -- with an article interviewing Ray Romano about the TV hit “Everybody Loves Raymond,” which was just coming into its own as a creative and commercial juggernaut. Over the years, he covered all areas of entertainment for the newspaper, including movies, music, books, and theater but especially TV. Michael Giltz became the newspaper’s first DVD critic (previously, the newspaper hadn’t regularly covered home video in any way). He was an early and avid supporter of “American Idol” – having seen the British version “Pop Idol” in the UK – and wrote about the show repeatedly for the Post, especially during the first two seasons, interviewing Simon Cowell and others connected with the phenomenon numerous times. Among his scoops for the New York Post, Michael Giltz was the first to break the news that Dave Chappelle was delaying and ultimately backing out of his wildly popular sketch show, a story that would make headlines around the world; broke the news on similar delays for the cult animated hit "The Boondocks," was the first to be told by Cowell that the judge would quit "Idol" if Fox insisted on airing the contest twice a year instead of just once (Fox backed down), garnered the first interview with teen idol Drake Bell of "Drake & Josh" after a near-fatal car accident and more.
