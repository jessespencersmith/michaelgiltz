Looks Dreamy in "Midsummer" A MIDSUMMER NIGHT'S DREAM ** 1/2 out of **** CLASSIC STAGE COMPANY The Classic Stage Company's production of Shakespeare's bauble A Midsummer Night's Dream has an eye-catching set and an eye-catching cast in various stages of undress.</p><p> If you've ever wanted to see an Abercrombie & Fitch model recite verse in theirunderwear (and who hasn't?), this is the show for you.</p><p> Run and catch it before it closes on May 20.</p><p> Butthe visage you're most likely to remember is that of the lithe and self-assured Bebe Neuwirth, commanding both physically and theatrically in dual roles as the fairy queen Titania and the slightly morose Hippolyta.</p><p> She moves with grace and speaks her lines with authority, matched moment to moment by Anthony Heald's Oberon/Theseus (a very lucky man, indeed).</p><p> I'd say Neuwirth is sexy despite the costumes of Andrea Lauer.</p><p> Shakespeare's tale of young lovers put at cross purposes by a mischief-making Puck (an over-exuberant Taylor Mac) is certainly one that encourages a free spirit in all areas.</p><p> But her costumes are a little broad and jokey for my taste, even in this context.</p><p> Still, Neuwirth looks wonderful, even in a silly leather dominatrix outfit.</p><p> I certainly won't fault Lauer for stripping the lovers down to their smalls.</p><p> It's a good way of distracting us from the fact that Christina Ricci, Nick Gehlfuss, Jordan Dean and Halley Wegryn Gross are all more confident cavorting about than turning the verse of Shakespeare into
