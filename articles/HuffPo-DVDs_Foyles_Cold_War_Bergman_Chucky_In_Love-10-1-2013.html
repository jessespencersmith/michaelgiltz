3 ($34.99 DVD; BBC) HARBOR COMMAND ($29.99 DVD; Timeless) Foyle's War is an acclaimed drama about a detective (Michael Kitchens in the role of a lifetime) who must continue his job on the home front during World War II when he'd rather be doing his bit.</p><p> Of course, you need police even during war so his job is vital and invariably includes lots of telling crimes (black market, Nazis and so on) that relate to the war.</p><p> It was always conceived to end with the war and to be frank, the show dragged it out a bit and the last season ortwo weren't always up to par.</p><p> And then, as so often happens, they said goodbye.</p><p> And then, as so often happens, they said hello again.</p><p> It's simply the best thing most everyone involved with this series has ever done.</p><p> So they've relaunched the series with Foyle involved in the Cold War, being drawn into MI5 against his better judgment.</p><p> Foyle fits smoothly into this deceptive, who-can-you-trust post-war atmosphere and the series feelsreborn in many ways.</p><p> It's as good as it's been in many a year.</p><p> The three TV movies in thisnew season are very strong and set the show up for many seasons to come.</p><p> They'll need to stop Foyle from being lied to by his superiors and Foyle unmasking them again and again.</p><p> That's run its course even in just three shows.</p><p> Creator Anthony Horowitz is clearly torn between calling it a day and ending on a high note (something they failed to do last time)and exploring the fresh new territory.</p><p> I say, commit to the Cold War, write somesmashing episodes and don't worry about the finish line.</p><p> Just tell great tells when you canfind them.</p><p> They have some great new characters and watching Foyle work with themcould be fun.</p><p> And for heaven's sake, bring back Foyle's son.</p><p> That wouldn't hurt a bit.</p><p> Garnered with Emmys, this TV movie about Liberace is mostly a draw for the performances of Michael Douglas as the pianist and Matt Damon as his lover.</p><p> What mighthave been disastrously campy somehow managed to be both serious and fun at the sametime.</p><p> Candelabra won't send people scurrying to unearth their old CDs of the performer, of course.</p><p> He sure could play but his main gift was showmanship.</p><p> Some people master theclassical canon.</p><p> Others prefer to glitter and be gay.</p><p> Not since Friday Night Lights has a great TV show collapsed so completely in its second season.</p><p> But like Friday Night Lights, Homeland has a strong enough cast that they have the ability to find their mojo again.</p><p> Mind you, the real problem may be that Homeland is a TV series that really should have been a miniseries.</p><p> Hopefully, they'll prove me wrong inSeason Three.</p><p> Both those shows won tons of Emmys (and Foyle's War has been garlanded overseas).</p><p> Don't be surprised if a lot of trophys get showered on The Hollow Crown next year.</p><p> It's a wildly ambitious miniseries that tackles Shakespeare's history plays with an all-star cast.</p><p> Sam Mendes, Richard Eyre and other theatrical heavyweights oversaw the project.</p><p> JeremyIrons, Tom Hiddleston and Ben Wishaw (as Richard II) star, alongside a bevy of greatslike Patrick Stewart, Simon Russel Beale, Julie Walters, John Hurt -- you get the idea.</p><p> You can't swing a stick without hitting a great actor.</p><p> This monumental work tackles four plays: Richard II, Henry IV Parts I and II and Henry V, which may cast the longest shadow since it's been filmed so successfully before.</p><p> It's airing on PBS right now and will surely beshown in classrooms for years to come.</p><p> Hey, if kids are trying to cheat and not read the plays, they could do worse.</p><p> Now we know the seventh series is the last for Matt Smith as Doctor Who (he has a few upcoming specials and then passes the torch at Christmas).</p><p> It's been a vaguelyunsatisfying final season.</p><p> His previous one had a major arc running throughout season sixthat was so ambitious and successful that it was inevitable that the next one would pale incomparison unless they raised the stakes again.</p><p> They didn't.</p><p> The goodbye to companionsAmy and Rory were a bit abrupt, though in fairness I'm not good with goodbyes.</p><p> Theholiday special ("The Doctor, The Widow And The Wardrobe" was very weak) and themystery of his Impossible Girl was both too built up and not bold enough.</p><p> They even had a few genuine clunkers.</p><p> I don't mean to go overboard.</p><p> The second half of the season was solid and Smith held his own throughout.</p><p> But looking back, I think the decision to bring in a new Doctor will let Smith walk away with his head held high.</p><p> They clearly ran out of steam and nothing jolts the place like a new lead actor.</p><p> God help Peter Capaldi.</p><p> So-so final season or not, Smith is a very hard act to follow.</p><p> If you want to catch up on previous Doctors, the special set The Doctors Revisited Fifth To Eighth is one of your most reasonably priced ways into the good Doctor's adventures.</p><p> Here you get a solid look at four Doctors (including Peter Davison, who I still think ofimmediately as a vet from All Creatures Great And Small ) and four complete adventures, one for each Doc.</p><p> Two rare-ish releases round out the Doctor Who titles this week.</p><p> The Ice Warriors is from the little mentioned years of Patrick Troughton.</p><p> The villains in this piece were included nicely in the season that just ended.</p><p> This is the first time they appeared.</p><p> It's rare because two of the six episodes were apparently lost for good.</p><p> They've been recreated via so-so animation and using the original voice soundtracks, a rather clever way to fill in the gaps that they've used before.</p><p> In contrast, Scream of the Shalka was a web only Luther is responsible for crimes himself.</p><p> Isn't it suspicious that Lutherseems to show up at so many crime scenes? (Um, well, he's a cop, so...no.) O'Hara delivershis sometimes ludicrous lines at a glacial pace that begins to have its own weirdfascination.</p><p> Never a great show, but even Elba can't do much with the material here.</p><p> Harbor Command was a syndicated half hour cop show that aired on TV stations back in 1957.</p><p> It's standard police stuff -- with a no-nonsense Dragnet vibe -- with one twist: all the crimes take
