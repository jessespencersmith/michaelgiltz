Cinema!" Or as my friend put it more simply,we're being called to prayer.</p><p> The Lumiere is jammed because today isthe premiere of Almodovar's Volver.</p><p> Will this be the year he finallywins the Palm d'Or?VOLVER *** or *** 1/2Maybe this will be his year.</p><p> Almodovar's movie gets solid applause andwill be the front runner throughout the festival.</p><p> Entertaining,satisfying.</p><p> With Almodovar, crazy events seem quite natural -- ghosts, adead body in the freezer, Penelope Cruz mopping floors -- it all seemsperfectly reasonable.</p><p> It plays like an old Hollywood movie thoughwithout any melodrama, with Cruz in the Joan Crawford role of amovie who has to take care of things when her daughter stabs herdead-beat dad after he tries to rape her. (She's adopted.) Men barelyregister in this film -- it's all about the women.</p><p> And Cruz's lovelybreasts deserve equal billing with her fine performance: onlyAlmodovar could have come up with the frankly appreciative overheadshot that shows Cruz washing dishes -- the only image in the frame isthe sink, the dishes, her hands and those wonderful breasts.</p><p> Almodovaris a momma's boy in the best sense of the phrase.BOFFO! *Apparently, when Stephen Spielberg was filming "Jaws," themechanical shark wasn't working and he had to come up with all themysterious, tension-building shots where the shark attacked but wedidn't see it -- the shots that made the film a classic.</p><p> And if thatanecdote is news to you, you might enjoy this supremely superficialdocumentary in whcih loads of Hollywood players are asked banalquestions about the industry. (I had a bet with friends about how longit would take someone to quote William Goldman's truism that inHollywood nobody knows anything.</p><p> I lost, because they saveddiscussion of that uber-quote for its own section at the 60 minutemark.) I swear the filmmakers held a gun to the heads of the peoplebeing interviewed and said, "Cliches only!
