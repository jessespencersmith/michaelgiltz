on.</p><p> Nonetheless, I found it a tad more plot-driven than most of his films and thus more engaging.</p><p> Plus, I'm a sucker for itinerant preachers and thelike.</p><p> The Guy (David Dewaele) is certainly saintly, quietly dropping to his knees in prayer at the drop of a hat.</p><p> He lives outdoors in a rural area of France, depending on The Girl (Alexandra Lamatre) for food and friendship, though he's not one much for talking.</p><p> When he takes a shotgun and kills her stepfather, wequickly realize she was being sexually molested.</p><p> Even her mother begs the Girl for forgiveness since shedidn't step in and put a stop to it.</p><p> Things get more puzzling when a local woman begs The Guy to see hercatatonic daughter whom the doctors can't help.</p><p> Turns out he's a Sin Eater of some sort, sucking out thegirl's demons and taking the burden upon himself.</p><p> This almost makes Hors Satan an action film by the standard of Dumont's movies.</p><p> Things get terribly murky in the third act when Dumont loses the threadof his story completely.</p><p> Everything we think we know about the Guy, the Girl and his special talents isconfounded in an uninteresting and messy manner.</p><p> Still, the two leads are strong, the visuals typicallystriking and for a while at least the plot was unexpectedly penetrable.</p><p> SNOWTOWN ** out of **** Many people had a
