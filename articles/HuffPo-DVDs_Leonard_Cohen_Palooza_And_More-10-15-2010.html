(The main image I took away was Cohen stepping back every once in a while bemused by the circus around him.) And it captures the oh so earnest tenor of the times beautifully.</p><p> Cohen invites fans to swarm towards thestage and then chastises security for freaking out.</p><p> Later, he thanks the "friend" who convinced him tosign away publishing rights to "Suzanne" because -- Cohen insists -- it wouldn't be right to make money off a song like that, a song that was such a gift and a privilege to compose.</p><p> Palmer is one of the great documentary filmmakers when it composes to music and opera, such as his landmark story of popularmusic, All You Need Is Love from the Seventies.</p><p> Here's another gem to his credit.</p><p> Go here to see the DVDs Palmer has available from his catalog.</p><p> SONGS FROM THE ROAD ($24.98 BluRay or $17.98 CD/DVD; Sony Legacy) -- Oh how times have changed.</p><p> Cohen has grown from thanking someone for pushing him into a bad publishing deal to havingto push himself out onto the road to make some money after another so-called friend bilked him out of funds and left Cohen broke.</p><p> But out of this sad circumstance came a heartening world tour in whichCohen played and sang with more assurance to more fans than ever before.</p><p> This concert picks and chooses the best performances (12 in all) from the entire tour, so a song from 2008 at the O2 Arena in London is followed by another one year later in San Jose, California.</p><p> It all flows smoothly because of arelative lack of unnecessary editing (by today's standards) and the eternal presence of Cohen and thatvoice and those lyrics.</p><p> Perhaps the biggest change is how his public persona has evolved so gracefully.</p><p> A man who has spent so many decades showered with praise might understandably be even more solemn and serious and full of himself.</p><p> Instead, Cohen is revealed as puckish, self-deprecating and generous ofheart time and again.</p><p> The result is a fine keepsake from probably the last tour of this magnitude he'll ever take. 1 of 5 Alan Grayson Gabby Giffords: A Few Words Dylan Ratigan A Destructive CrossroadsDON'T MISS HUFFPOST BLOGGERS HOT TRENDS camille grammer helen mirren poses nude miley cyrus lap dance video natalie portman natalie portman pregnant PHOTOS: Raven-Symone's Dramatic Weight Loss More Celebrity News at People.com Like 720 Congresswoman Gabrielle Giffords In 'Very Critical Condition' After Shooting READ MORE Kate Middleton JoinsFuture In-Laws at Weekend Wedding READ MORE BUZZ: Are Zac Efron &Vanessa Hudgens Back On?
