She even tells the classic anecdote about the very first song that Pop Staples taught to his children after being disgusted with the no-shows at rehearsal for his gospel group.</p><p> That first song? "Will The Circle Be Unbroken." Will she sing it? Yes, of course she will.</p><p> The crowd at the sold-out show in Brooklyn ate it up, perhaps since many of them were seeing Staples in concert for the first time.</p><p> Her every reference to Jeff Tweedy (who oversaw her latest album, one of themost acclaimed of the year and gave Staples a lovely new standard wit the title track "You Are Not Alone") was greeted with applause.</p><p> Calling him a funny little thing pleased them even more.</p><p> MOST POPULAR ON HUFFPOST 1 of 2FOLLOW HUFFINGTON POST BIG NEWS: James Franco | Movies | Rihanna | George Clooney | Energy Debates | More...</p><p> FINAL COUNTDOWN Maddow, Bill Maher React To Olbermann's MSNBC Exit Oprah To Reveal Huge Family Secret Monday Raoul Felder On Celebrity Splits, Divorce News Of The Day Howard Fineman: 'Loss Of Steam' In GOP Health Care Repeal Efforts Signs That Your 'Loving Relationship' May Be An Addiction Professor Targeted By Beck Gets Death Threats Michelle Bombshell To KatLOG IN | SIGN UP SHARE THIS STORY Submit this story Get Entertainment Alerts Sign Up Like 18K Like 912 Like 892 Like 72 Like 145 Like 527 Recommend 3K It was an enthusiastic crowd but they didn't quite feel the gospel numbers in their bones; yes, they hooted and made some embarrassing (if sincere) stabs at some "amens" and "uh-huhs." A sold out show at Irving Plaza and then the Beacon, I assume.</p><p> They definitely put on a tight, satisfying show that outshines their fine full-length debut, which boasts tunes like the title track "Pickin' Up The Pieces" and "Breakin' The Chains Of Love."
