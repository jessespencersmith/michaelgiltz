Every single time I put it on I got chills and thegorgeous vocals, stirring arrangements and wave of emotions they brought out in me.</p><p> Are there albumsjust as good from 2011? (Seriously, I've been writing this list while Diamond Mine plays and it's breaking my heart with the vulnerability and empathy on display.</p><p> Great melodies.</p><p> Simple but somehow perfect vocals with just the right sonic touches.</p><p> A real keeper.) 3.</p><p> TOM WAITS Bad As Me (bohemian troubadour in top form) Just one song kept the great Waits from the top.</p><p> I've liked so much of what he's done over the years, including the industrial strength clamor of much of his work in the past decade.</p><p> Here his sonics are more restrained so fans who haven't visitedWaits in a long time should dive back in.</p><p> This is one of his strongest works in years, from the typicallyclanging opener "Chicago" (it settles down soon enough) to the heart-breaking closer "New Year's Eve,"the sort of drunken elegy Waits does so well we take it for granted.</p><p> So what song spoiled it ever soslightly? "Last Leaf On The Tree," a fine little ditty that simply repeats itself to distraction.</p><p> At two minutes this slight tune would go by quickly.</p><p> At four minutes it's a bit of a distraction given the limited melody on display.</p><p> Keep this in perspective: I was niggled by a few bars of one song on an otherwisemarvelous work that shows Waits as deeply in command as ever.</p><p> Like Neil Young and Bob Dylan andother greats, time is not dimming his talent in the least.4.</p><p> GILLIAN WELCH The Harrow and the Harvest (simple, straightforward, striking folk) Well, if Welch is gonna make us wait years and years for a new album, at least she delivers the goods and then some.</p><p> This follow-up to Soul Journey is impeccable folk-rock.</p><p> It could just as easily have been my Album of the Year. 5.</p><p> TEDDY THOMPSON Bella / kd lang Sing It Loud /RON SEXSMITH -- Long Player, Late Bloomer (pure pop by pure pros) No, I'm not consistent.</p><p> Sometimes I get annoyed when the music press genuflects in front of the latest offering from the Rolling Stones, Bruce Springsteen, et al in almost an unconscious reflex.</p><p> On the other hand, when they ignore terrific new music by established acts that aren'tat the superstar level, I want them to pay attention.</p><p> Maybe these artists simply don't have heat.</p><p> TeddyThompson hasn't broken through commercially despite getting better and better with every album, somaybe the press has written him off as an act that can't sell magazines. kd lang hasn't come out in awhile, so what new can you say about her? And we all know Sexsmith is an impeccable pop craftsman;didn't Elvis Costello tell us so for years? Nonetheless, all three have delivered absolutely top-notch pop albums.</p><p> If you've ever been a fan, don't hesitate for a moment.6.</p><p> WYNTON MARSALIS AND ERIC CLAPTON Play the Blues / BRANFORD MARSALIS AND JOEY CALDARAZZO Songs of Mirth And Melancholy (trad jazz) Surely the Marsalis brothers hate having all their work linked.</p><p> Oh well.</p><p> Wynton has found new purpose and a sense of joy via collaborations with the likes of Willie Nelson and now Clapton.</p><p> Their jam session brings out the best in both of them.(Who knew "Layla" would prove so malleable?) And does anyone in jazz have a more gorgeous tone thatBranford? For sheer sonic beauty, it's the best since Getz.</p><p> HIs album is also a collaboration and it leansmore heavily on melancholy than mirth.</p><p> Great stuff.7.</p><p> GLEN CAMPBELL Ghost on the Canvas (haunting country pop) I have no strong affection for Glen Campbell and won't try and retroactively make his career more important than it deserves.</p><p> I think a strong greatest hits album sums him up very well, which is hardly something to sneer at.</p><p> Many artistsnever get even that.</p><p> So his illness and late period Cash-like recording don't tug at my heartstrings for anyreason other than the music.</p><p> But what music.</p><p> Campbell's voice -- his greatest weapon -- isn't remotely asgood as in his heyday but now we can appreciate what a terrific singer he always was.</p><p> Like Elvis Presley'salbum Elvis Country (I'm 10,000 Years Old) , this combines terrific songs with spoken word passages to spooky, marvelous effect. 8.</p><p> BOMBINO Agadez (Tuareg rocks!) / BOUBACAR TRAORE Mali Denhou (gentle African guitar) / LADYSMITH BLACK MAMBAZO Songs From a Zulu Farm (ecstatic and playful children's music) Bombino is a guitarist par excellence, a superstar in Africa who draws on traditional sounds along with rock and blues while delivering the seemingly endless pleas for peace and hope that Africa invariablyneeds but lacks.</p><p> He surely acknowledges a debt to Traore, a fellow African who suffered not fromrepression but by seeming linked to oppressors and being out of favor for years because of it.</p><p> Happily, they can find common ground in their music.</p><p> Then there's Ladysmith Black Mambazo, an act you knowand -- if you've seen them in concert -- love.</p><p> This album of children's songs they learned as kids growingup in rural South Africa is one of their lightest and most sheerly entertaining in their entire discography. 9.</p><p> VARIOUS ARTISTS This May Be My Last Time Singing: Raw African American Gospel on 45 RPM (1957-1982) (lo-fi, high flying gospel that's so good you'll convert) This followup to the excellent compilation Fire in My Bones is another revelation.</p><p> Filled with well-known tunes and obscure songs performed generally by acts only the most devoted gospel listener has heard of (no Kirk Franklin or Mahalia here), it's a delight from beginning to end.</p><p> The so-so sound quality of many of these raritiesactually works to the album's benefit.</p><p> You feel like you're listening to them via a transistor radio catchinga far-away station or maybe down the block from a house of worship.</p><p> The performances are joyous,foreboding, ecstatic and majestic.10.</p><p> ADELE 21 (unstoppable soul diva) A great album with a string of singles that get better and better the more you hear them -- that's about as great as pop music gets.</p><p> Beyonce and Lady Gaga had some great singles, but they couldn't come within a mile of the genuine artistic statement of this album.</p><p> Here's hoping Adele stays healthy vocally and unhealthy in her choice of men.</p><p> Yep, it's Mary J.</p><p> Blige all overagain -- do we really want to risk Adele writing about how content she is? Don't worry; we'll be there tocomfort you. 11.</p><p> GROUPLOVE Never Trust a Happy Song (raucous L.A. pop-rock) Why this L.A. band isn't the toast of the town simply escapes me.</p><p> Terrific stuff. 12.</p><p> PANIC! AT THE DISCO Vices & Virtues (sterling but overlooked pop-rock) Am I a sucker for pure pop? Panic! can add or drop the exclamation point as many times as they want as long as they keepcreating indelible melodies and stirring rock like they do here. 13.</p><p> NICK LOWE The Old Magic (vintage wine, vintage bottle) I've never been deeply invested in Nick Lowe but boy is this a charmer.</p><p> The only reason it isn't higher on my list is because I haven't listened to it enough yet.</p><p> This video isn't from his current album.</p><p> But it's recent and shows Lowe in excellent form. 14.</p><p> WILD FLAG Wild Flag (giving super groups a good name) Sleater-Kinney lives in this new band composed of some of the talent from that group and their friends.</p><p> Unruly fun.15.</p><p> BRAD MEHLDAU Modern Music (dependably adventurous jazz trio)/ COLIN VALLON -- Rruger (bold EU trio following in Mehldau's wake) Mehldau is consistently great so the strength of his latest is no surprise.</p><p> Now his influence is seeping out to the continent, not by the obvious tactic of covering unexpected pop songs but simply in their commitment to the piano-led jazz trio. 16.</p><p> JAY-Z AND KANYE WEST Watch the Throne (braggadocio taken to new heights) Neither is quite my cup of tea but they've delivered great works before.</p><p> West's last album was the first one I truly loved.</p><p> Together they indulge in the usual hip-hop obnoxiousness of boasting and putting down bitches, but somehow they're in such good spirits here it kind of won me over.17.</p><p> BON IVER Bon Iver (stares down success with quiet confidence) Bon Iver's intense publicity surrounding his last album didn't corner him into repeating himself.</p><p> This album gently expands his sonic palette.</p><p> I thought maybe he was the flavor of the week, but who knows? He might just be in it for the long haul. 18.</p><p> BALLAKE SISSOKO AND VINCENT SEGAL Chamber Music (delicate instrumentals) I could look up all sorts of facts about who they are, where they come from, their instruments and the like.</p><p> But the simple fact is that their album is lovely. 19.</p><p> PAUL SIMON So Beautiful or So What (mortality, musically) I can't remember the last time I put a Paul Simon album on my best of the year last. (Probably Rhythm of the Saints. ) And I love the guy! As one of the all-time greats, I hold him to a higher standard.</p><p> And he meets it here on this pop gem aboutfacing death with wit, resignation and some excellent melodies. 20.</p><p> BETH HART & JOE BONAMASSA Don't Explain (the blues, thumpingly good) The blues make for a stomping good time when performed with such glee. 21.</p><p> PRESERVATION HALL JAZZ BAND AND DEL MCCOURY BAND American Legacies (an institution finds new life via collaboration) PHJB is an institution and as such, much of its best work was probably behind it.</p><p> Until 2010 when it collaborated with an all-star cast to celebrate some anniversary oranother and the band produced one of its most enjoyable albums in years.</p><p> One of those collaboratorsproved so successful, they went and recorded an entire album.</p><p> The results do not disappoint.22.</p><p> AMBROSE AKINMUSIRE When the Heart Emerges Glistening (quietly probing jazz) A sterling Blue Note debut by one of jazz's best young trumpeters.</p><p> A clutch of strong originals, a great young quintet, focus,vision -- this is very exciting indeed.</p><p> For a live performance from his new album, you must go to YouTube . 23.</p><p> JAMES BLAKE James Blake (trippy pop) Not my thing usually, but Blake has enough snippets of emotion to keep me returning and every time I do it seems a little more cohesive and satisfying. 24.</p><p> VARIOUS ARTISTS Live From the Old Town School (folk's beating heart) Sometimes you go out to track down history.</p><p> Other times you can wait quietly and have it come to you.</p><p> The Chicago center of folk Old Town School has seen countless legends pass through its doors.</p><p> Apparently they knew it andrecorded everyone who came to town.</p><p> This 4 CD set contains a truckload of inspired performances fromTaj Mahal and Mahalia Jackson to Jeff Tweedy and Peter Case.</p><p> A fourth CD devoted to world musicdoesn't have quite the same impact but the overall level of quality and the mix of great and unexpectedmake this a genuine discovery.</p><p> Here's a performance (not from the album) by Steve Dawson at thelegendary venue.25.</p><p> GIRLS Father, Son, Holy Ghost (interesting identity crisis) I loved the first album by Girls but was slightly suspicious nonetheless.</p><p> Now their proper followup has me even more nervous.</p><p> Each song seems to come from an entirely different band.</p><p> Do Girls even know what sort of sound they're going for, if any?It shouldn't work but the album holds together for me out of sheer perversity.</p><p> You have no idea what toexpect but it's all good.</p><p> I hope they find more direction next time but for the moment the grab-bagapproach is working just fine. 26.</p><p> TINARIWEN Tassili (bluesy, distinctive, campfire sing-alongs) One of the strongest acts in world music for the past few years, Tinariwen don't disappoint with their latest.</p><p> I always picture them performing around a campfire in the middle of the desert, the flames the only light for miles around? AmI romanticizing or fetishizing them? Maybe.</p><p> But their music is bluesy and distinctive enough to travelaround the globe so I'm sure it'll withstand my flight of fancy.27.</p><p> JOY FORMIDABLE The Big Roar (noisy pop) A child of Nirvana, they have its gift for making an unholy racket but keeping it reined in with a very strong pop sense.</p><p> Very good stuff. 28.</p><p> DARI0 MARINELLI WITH JACK LIEBECK Jane Eyre soundtrack (the year's best traditional score)/ MATTHEW COOPER Some Days Are Better Than Others (the year's best untraditional score) The score for the fine remake of Jane Eyre is the best orchestral score of the year.</p><p> I haven't even seen the indie film Some Days Are Better Than Others , but the pop instrumental score by Matthew Cooper is so stirring and lovely it certainly makes me want to right away.29.</p><p> THE CORAL Butterfly House /THE MAGIC NUMBERS Runaway (pop, unimported, unrecognized) I kept waiting for both of these albums to be released in the U.S. but they never were.</p><p> Both bands are more appreciated in the U.K. than they are here.</p><p> The Coral live for nuggets-style garage rock while Magic Numbers savor the gorgeous vocals of Laurel Canyon.</p><p> Multiple tracks should be bigradio hits. 30.</p><p> PISTOL ANNIES Pistol Annies / MIRANDA LAMBERT Four The Record (country's top gal and friends) Miranda Lambert -- the SECOND runner-up on the late, lamented (by me) friends to fill the gaping hole where the Dixie Chicksused to be.</p><p> Combine her best work on both of these album and you've got a great work indeed. 31.</p><p> THE LOW ANTHEM Smart Flesh (brainy Americana) Still waiting to break through to a wider audience a la Avett Brothers and Mumford & Sons.</p><p> That good. 32.</p><p> CHARLIE HADEN AND QUARTET WEST Sophisticated Ladies (female singers, for the ride.33.</p><p> THE BLACK KEYS El Camino (rock, no fuss) I respected and admired and appreciated The Black Keys but never quite dug them until last week when I was walking down Fifth Avenue (one of the least Black Keys-appropriate streets in NYC) when it just clicked and I could groove on their music.Sometimes it just takes the right frame of (an open) mind. 34.</p><p> M83 Hurry Up, We're Dreaming (ambient pop) Another act where I need the right setting.</p><p> In their case, it usually involves a late night, an open highway and the perfect number, like "Raconte, Moi Une appreciate the Krauss for a while because the CD came "burdened" with bonus tracks that made the CD too long and kept me from appreciating the album proper for quite a while.</p><p> It took my friend Sal at Burning Wood to nudge me into giving the Hiatt a second chance and realizing he's strung together one of his best sincethat miracle heyday around Bring the Family.36.</p><p> BEASTIE BOYS Hot Sauce Committee, Part Two (feisty hip-hop from the old-timers) They've still got life in them yet. 37.</p><p> BEN HOWARD Every Kingdom (gentle pop)/ MIKE BLOOM King Of Circles (WARM singer- songwriter vibe for this solo debut) Two quiet works of solid tunes that get better with each listen.38.</p><p> VINICIUS CANTUARIA AND BILL FRISELL Lagrimas Mexicanas (two great guitars, one great voice equal haunting Latin music) A playful, delightful collaboration between the always adventurous Frisell and the Brazilian singer and instrumentalist Cantuaria.</p><p> Really charming. 39.</p><p> THE GOURDS Old Mad Joy (rootsy celebration) / MARC BROUSSARD Marc Broussard (bluesyrock) Americana from the front porch, for then Gourds, and from the juke joint for Broussard, who has it all to be a star except the touring and radio that once upon a time would have championed him. 40.</p><p> SUZANNE VEGA Close-Up Volume 3: State Of Being (acoustic songs, electric songwriting) Vega has been recording her catalog over again so she can own the masters to these new versions and profit from them at least a little (is that asking so much for an artist?).</p><p> Out of necessity has come fresh proofabout her exceptional catalog of songs and Vega's gifts as a performer.</p><p> After her debut, Vega quicklyproved she was no folkie, thanks to an adventurous gift for sonic experimentation and great pop sense.But here she is stripping her songs down again mainly to a voice and a guitar.</p><p> The results are gripping,mixing older and fresher tunes side by side till all you can see is the impressive breadth of her career.Plus each volume works beautifully on its own terms.41.</p><p> VARIOUS ARTISTS Those Shocking, Shaking Days: Indonesia Hard, Psychedelic, Progressive Rock and Funk (1970-1978) (rock and roll knows no borders) Truly, rock and roll is everywhere and always has been.</p><p> If there's a better example of world music than Indonesian acts pounding out these thumping originals back in the 1970s while singing lyrics like "I like the Rolling Stones," well I don't know it.</p><p> Is there no end to the riches produced around the world we're barely aware of much less listened
