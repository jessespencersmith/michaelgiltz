Learn About the Great Lakes, Fighting Zika, Eclipses & More in 24 New Books!***************GOODBYE, PAULA FOX Novelist Paula Fox died last week at the age of 93.</p><p> In a typically evocative and perceptive New York Times obituary by Margalit Fox (no relation), she points out that Fox was the rare writer who had penned books people could read from birth to death, including picture books and middle grade books and fiction and memoirs, all of it acclaimed.</p><p> The autobiography Borrowed Finery was a late career capper for the woman who would die at 93, a nice final act in a life that began so disastrously.</p><p> As the obituary states in a style the writer herself would have admired, "Paula Fox was born in Manhattan on April 22, 1923 to parents who did not want her." Her relative obscurity in recent years changed when author Jonathan Franzen became obsessed with Fox's brilliant novel about a dissolving marriage -- Desperate Characters -- and talked it up to one and all.</p><p> There's no better way to celebrate a person's life than to delve into what they created.</p><p> So check out Desperate Characters and her other books right away. *************** ILL WILL by Dan Chaon This is not for the fainthearted. "Ill Will" combines a violent murder set in the past with the chilling revelation of a serial killer stalking young college men in the present.</p><p> There is no super detective or dedicated FBI agent who will save the day in either case.</p><p> No.</p><p> There is no relief from this novel’s remorse or psychological rage.</p><p> And retribution will have its way.</p><p> In fact, ill will stalks the novel’s characters like a cold fog seeping into every corner of their lives.</p><p> For its probing character insight and its expansive analysis of how we think about crime, this is a true novel with an American crime landscape, from the plains of Nebraska to the strip malls of suburban Ohio to the farmhouses of Oregon....</p><p> Read More.</p><p> Love Thrillers & Mysteries?
