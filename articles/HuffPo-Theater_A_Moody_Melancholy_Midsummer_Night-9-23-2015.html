a clump of humanity, somehow disentangled themselves and then were drawnineluctably back together again, all the while giving us the story we know so well.Two young lovers are denied the possibility of happiness by the woman's implacable father.</p><p> He insists shemarry the young man he prefers instead.</p><p> Rather than marrying a man she doesn't love or face death by royaldecree or worse (a nunnery!), the two meet in the forest and plan to spirit away to another land.</p><p> She is pursuedby the man betrothed to her by her apparently bi-curious father (one idea of many that go nowhere) while thatman in turn is pursued by another young woman who adores him with slavish -- and thus unwelcome --devotion.</p><p> In the forest, all four would-be lovers are set upon by fairies who delight in toying with the vagaries oflove.Wandering about this rather crowded forest we also find some local workers who fancy themselves a troupe ofactors.</p><p> They are rehearsing a play in hopes of performing it for the king during his wedding nuptials.</p><p> Thus theymay curry favor and perhaps even make their (modest) fortune.</p><p> Fairies being fairies, the actors too are set upon.A
