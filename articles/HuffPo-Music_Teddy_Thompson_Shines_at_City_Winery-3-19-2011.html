Thompson's mordant wit was on full display at City Winery for an acoustic show that ran a substantial 2 hours and 10 minutes but still left many of his best tunes waiting to be played.</p><p> His dry humor was a constant, from claiming he walked up to drunken Irish people in Times Square on St.</p><p> Patrick's Day tosay, "No, I WON'T kiss you" to describing what was happening across the street during the concert (thestage faces the audience and behind them huge windows looking out on Varick so anyone performingenjoys a backdrop of taxis and pedestrians).</p><p> And of course repeated jokes about his lack of album sales.When Thompson commented on his inability to remember some of the lyrics to his new songs, a fanshouted out that he could fake it and they wouldn't notice. "Because you haven't bought the album," shotback Thompson. "Sales reflect that," he added when people dissented. "Don't worry about me; I'm rollingin it.</p><p> Oil money!" It's only a small exaggeration to say people come to hear Thompson as much for hishumorous patter as his music.</p><p> And there was plenty of music to enjoy, from the rollicking "Looking For A Girl" to "Delilah" and "Over and Over" and "Home" and "Gotta Have Someone," all from his excellent new album Bella .</p><p> As on this in- studio performance for WNYC's SoundCheck, Thompson performed solo with just an acoustic guitar.</p><p> You can stream all the songs from Bella via his page on MySpace, but you might as well buy the album now.</p><p> It gets better and better with every listen.</p><p> The show was billed as Teddy Thompson & Friends.</p><p> Without resisting the urge to put himself down (he has no friends, suggests you be nice to friends and co-workers because someday you might need their help, etc.) Teddy then called up Rufus Wainwright to duet on "King of The Road," the Roger Miller classicthey performed on the soundtrack to Brokeback Mountain. ( Here's an amusing video of them doing it in the UK accompanied by ukulele. ) Despite holding the lyrics in his hand, Rufus bungled some of the lyrics (which he seems to always do on this song), but it only added to the casual, friendly air of the evening.</p><p> Their voices blend together so well, you can't help thinking they should do a duets album.</p><p> But the same thought crosses your mind when Teddy sang a few songs with Jenni Muldaur, one of his favorite singers Striking Distance Of Obama Popeater: Brad and Angelina's Strict Nanny Requirements Shelterpop: Unplugging Appliances: Do I REALLY Have to Do This?
