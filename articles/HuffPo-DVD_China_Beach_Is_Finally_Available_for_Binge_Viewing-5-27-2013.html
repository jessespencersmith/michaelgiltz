DVD: China Beach Is Finally Available for Binge Viewing It seems like every TV show ever made is out on DVD or streaming on demand.</p><p> But that's far from true.</p><p> China Beach is the latest show fans have been eagerly anticipating to finally get the boxed set it deserves.</p><p> But many more shows are still waiting.</p><p> Where's all of HillStreet Blues and I'll Fly Away and The Wonder Years and St.</p><p> Elsewhere, just to name a few? CHINA BEACH THE COMPLETESERIES ($199.95 DVD; TimeLife) - Well, it took forever (blame the music) but whenthey finally did it, they did it right.</p><p> Belovedcult drama China Beach is finally out in agorgeous boxed set that includes all 62episodes on 21 discs, along with hours andhours of reunions, bonus features,commentary tracks and more.</p><p> It's allhoused in an impressive and handsomebox that's attractive but compact which fitseasily alongside the other shows in yourcollection.</p><p> For now, the set is availabledirectly from TimeLife though eventually it will come to other outlets.</p><p> Why did it take so long? The songs.</p><p> The show was bursting with pop music of the era and it's very hard to get clearances to use them again on DVD. (Any music publisher/artist who demands exorbitant amounts is an idiot, by the way.) It's the same reason The Wonder Years is lost in limbo.</p><p> China Beach was sort of a serious spin on M*A*S*H, the comedy set in Korea. it was an hour long drama that looked at the nurses and doctors and patients surrounding a MASH unit located near a great beach complete with lifeguard.</p><p> The iconic opening scene showedDana Delany looking very sexy as she sat out in the sun in a red bathing suit, only to havea helicopter zoom overhead and the audience realize she's a nurse in a war zone.</p><p> The brieffirst season was earnest and awkward, due to unfortunate comic relief in a USO singer-wannabe.</p><p> But John Wells (of ER) took the reins in season two and the show grew by leapsand bounds.</p><p> The series would score three nominations for Best Drama and Delany wouldwin Best Actress twice.</p><p> It was never close to a popular hit, which was all to the good.</p><p> Like St.</p><p> Elsewhere, it was a critics' darling that stayed under the radar and could do what it wanted.</p><p> The show becameincreasingly bold, using complicated structures for some scenes, flash-forwards to life May 9, 2014 This is the print preview: Back to normal view Â» Posted: 05/27/2013 6:10 pmafter these soldiers returned home, documentary interviews with nurses and others interspersed with fictional scenes and even a cast visit to the Vietnam Memorial.</p><p> I haven't finished watching it yet but have the feeling they could have gone one more year to fully tackle the stateside lives of these veterans and Colleen McMurphy in particular.But there's a lot of TV here and they continued to get bolder and better and how manyshows can say that?
