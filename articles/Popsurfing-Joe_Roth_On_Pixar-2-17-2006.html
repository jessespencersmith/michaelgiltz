(Roth has been a studio honcho and film producer anddirector and sits on the board of Pixar.) He makes two odd assertions,however.</p><p> First, Roth says he wouldn't be surprised if five years downthe road Disney was releasing two animated movies a year -- one aPixar original and the other a Pixar sequel.</p><p> That's pretty dumb since itmeans Disney would abandon the Disney brand and never make anymovies outside the Pixar aesthetic, like stop-motion or hand-drawnmovies.</p><p> Pixar's people are too smart to think there's only one "type" ofanimated movie to make.</p><p> Pixar movies don't succeed because they'reCGI; they work because they are creative and have solid stories.Second, Roth says we can expect sequels to The Incredibles, Toy Story,Monsters Inc., Little Nemo and so on.</p><p> Hasn't Roth been listening?
