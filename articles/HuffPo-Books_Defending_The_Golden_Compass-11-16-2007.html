Pullman himself is most certainly an atheist and no fan organized religion in general.</p><p> But his books are most scathing towards dogmatism and unquestioned authority, not any one faith.</p><p> There are brave (gay) angels in later volumes -- which must have thrown Donohue into conniptions if he got that far --not to mention the death of a God-like figure, and a church that is kidnapping and torturingchildren in an obsessive desire to rub out the "sin" of sexuality.</p><p> It's bold and compelling andultimately very challenging indeed -
