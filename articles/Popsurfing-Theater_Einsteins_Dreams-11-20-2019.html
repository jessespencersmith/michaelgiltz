WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER 20, 2019 THEATER: "Einstein's Dreams" Too Elusive For Reality EINSTEIN'S DREAMS * 1/2 out of **** PROSPECT THEATER COMPANY AT 59E59 Alan Lightman's charming novel Einstein's Dreams was a best-selling sensation in 1992.</p><p> Lightman's day job is a physicist and his first and best work of fiction playfully combines both disciplines.</p><p> In it, heimagines Einstein is working on his Theory of Relativity and dreams about the various ways one can think about time.</p><p> What if time really did stand still? What if time proceeded in circles? Or skipped andstuttered or reversed itself unexpectedly? Each dream of time iscaptured in a fragmentary passage lasting just a few pages at most.</p><p> It'snot really a novel or even a collection of short stories, but more like a variation on a theme.</p><p> Lightman lets you feel you grasp certain abstract scientific ideas, all while entertaining you thoroughly.</p><p> But there's no plot to speak of, noover-arching thread.</p><p> It's just fanciful fun.</p><p> Even the packaging of the book added to its appeal: Einstein's Dream was about 5 inches wide by 7 inches tall, almost a square, friendly little book.</p><p> It fit into your hands comfortably and made this Italo Calvino-like work quite approachable.Instead of thinking, a book about physics and running away, you sawit and smiled and picked it up.</p><p> I read it, I enjoyed it and not for a single moment did I think, this should be adapted for the stage! It's one of those works that wouldseem to resist a transformation into film or theater or TV.</p><p> Of course, Ifelt the same about Milan Kundera's The Unbearable Lightness of Being.</p><p> Turn that daunting, philosophical novel into a film? Absurd! Turn the classic Powell-Pressburger movie A Matter Of Life and Death into a stage play? Ridiculous! Well, as a film, Unbearable became another masterpiece.</p><p> As a play, A Matter Of Life and Death was a disaster.</p><p> But hey, they tried!
