you're studying nature and come across an unexpected creature or observe unusual behavior, you shouldn't get distracted by anything else.</p><p> Keep your focus on the prizeyou've discovered.</p><p> That's a lesson for playwright ThomasHiggins, who has nailed down some fresh relationships and intriguing moments in his new play Wild Animals You Should Know .</p><p> But instead of focusing in on those interesting new creatures, he gets distracted by the ordinary and mundane.</p><p> The heart of his new drama is the odd friendship between Jacob (Gideon Glick) and Matthew (Jay Armstrong Johnson).</p><p> In one of several marvelous scenes, the show begins with Jacob and Matthew chatting online via video.</p><p> Matthew is dressed in his full Scout uniform and reciting the Boy Scout code as he sexily removes the cap and scarf and then unbuttons his shirt in a slow strip tease.</p><p> It's Jacob's birthdayand Matthew has promised the full monty.</p><p> Jacob is gay while Matthew is straight-ish but enjoying the attention of Jacob's desire and, yes, getting off on it himself.</p><p> When Matthew says he's not gay but it just so happens that Jacob "gives really good blow-jobs," you might wonder who is kidding who.</p><p> Still, whenMatthew insists he's just narcissistic, it rings true.</p><p> Their dynamic -- of friendship and sex, of an ostensibly straight kid dominating and yet needing the approval of a gay kid -- is a rich one.</p><p> During that first scene, Matthew realizes a neighbor has been watching his strip-tease.</p><p> Turning the EDITION: U.S.</p><p> Not in the least.</p><p> Later diversions from the heart of the matter include Jacob's rambling tale to Walter of how he and Matthew reignited their friendship after years of drifting apart or scenes of Scoutmaster Rodney dealing with neighborhood abuse.</p><p> That merely reminds us that we should know more about Jacob and Matthew's relationship.</p><p> They chat online but does Matthew ignore Jacob in school? Or has he truly befriended him?We just don't know and that's not right.</p><p> Other questions abound.</p><p> It's strange: Walter realizes his son Matthew has probably done questionable things but instead of apologizing to Rodney he wants to know why the hell a gay man would join the Scouts, as if the very actwere suspicious.</p><p> The reason propounded by the playwright is a vague cliche about a dead lover who wasan Artist.</p><p> But what's the mystery? Unless one believes a gay man can't be around teenage boys without preying on them, why is it so puzzling that an adult who loves the outdoors and wants to share that appreciation would become a Scout leader? Can't Rodney just say he loves the Scouts and wanted tocontinue the tradition and was willing to accept the humiliating situation of having to lie in order to do so? It was a sacrifice he as an adult was ready to make.</p><p> We can see Jacob truly in love with Scouting as well, so it's clear how this could happen.</p><p> Instead, something darker might possibly be at work, as raised in the suggestive climax that is partially unearned; in a better play, it would lead to characters grappling with the questions raised, rather thanjust raising them.</p><p> Still, director Trip Cullman scores in the scenes where something fascinating is going on and adds detail and polish to the scenes that don't.</p><p> The scenic design by Andromache Chalfant makes effective use of video projection of stylized settings to suggest the anonymity of the suburbs and thestarkness of nature.</p><p> The costumes of Jenny Mannis are spot-on, though Scout uniforms are the sexiestthing on display.</p><p> Fitz Patton's sound design usefully sets the mood, especially in the key camping moments.</p><p> And the lighting by David Weiner is very strong throughout, capturing the subtle shifts in mood and the dangerous switch from intimate to intimacy, from sexy to scary.</p><p> MOST POPULAR ON HUFFPOST 1 of 2 Fox News Viewers Know Less Than People Who Watch No News: Study Loyal Dog Refuses To Leave Owner's Grave, Goes Week Without Food Charlie Angus What if They Declared an Emergency and No One Came?
