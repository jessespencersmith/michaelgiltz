ROYAL PAINS SEASON THREE VOLUME ONE ($26.98; Universal) By and large, American television does a TERRIBLE job releasing their new shows on DVD.</p><p> In the UK, TV shows and movies and miniseries come out on DVD the minute the current season of the show hasended, sometimes even before.</p><p> In America, the studios rather idiotically have decided to wait until rightbefore the new season has begun to release the previous season.</p><p> This has two results: one, most peoplewriting about TV write about the new season, rather than the old season coming out on DVD six monthsafter it ended.</p><p> Two, fans who might want to "join" a show by catching up can't do so because the previousseason comes out so close to the new season.</p><p> Even worse are kids shows, which often see a single season broken up into multi-DVD releases that make them expensive and annoying to collect.</p><p> HBO has added a new wrinkle to this.</p><p> They want to encourage people to subscribe to HBO and access their entire libraryvia HBO To Go, so they delayed the DVD release of their high profile series Boardwalk Empire (with the pilot directed by executive producer Martin Scorsese) until one month after the finale of Season TWO! This is a lavish series with a top-notch cast and a very familiar story of gangsters and the feds during Prohibition.</p><p> I can't speak to its quality yet since I'm still catching up with episodes that aired some 16months ago and many people have said the show didn't catch fire until season two.</p><p> But it's certainly well-acted and intelligent.</p><p> But HBO has it wrong.</p><p> The more people that watch their shows the more likely theywill be to subscribe.</p><p> Hiding a show from DVD for a year longer than necessary goes against everything we've learned about creative content: make it available to consumers any way they want, whether it's digital, on a tablet, onBluRay, on demand, or via their cable subscription.</p><p> Keeping Boardwalk Empire from DVD doesn't make HBO more desirable.</p><p> It keeps their shows less well-known and therefore less important.</p><p> For 16 months, Ihaven't even been able to discuss Boardwalk Empire or read a single story to avoid spoilers.</p><p> How is that a good idea? Season two debuted to lower numbers than the season one finale.</p><p> On the bright side, it's only $60 retail on DVD, far lower than HBO's usual premium price.</p><p> No one else has gone as extreme as HBO but they don't do it right, either.</p><p> Anyone wanting to catch up with one of the wittiest action dramas on TV -- Timothy Olyphant's Justified -- has one week to watch 13 hours before season three debuts on January 17 on FX.</p><p> I can't quite get over the idea that Shameless is an unnecessary remake of a raunchy UK show.</p><p> But it has a fun cast and I've been hearing good buzz.Maybe I would have caught up with it before season two debuted on Showtime last Sunday, and if I'dwatched one episode a night since the DVD was released on December 27, I could have just barely donethat.</p><p> Imagine if it had come out right after the season one finale, just over nine months ago.</p><p> Finally, the USA network show Royal Pains is good junk food with a doctor catering to the filthy rich in the Hamptons.</p><p> But now they've split up the season into two sets, releasing volume one now and volume two later.</p><p> Fans don't care about scheduling.</p><p> In this case, 10 episodes aired from June through August andanother six will be broadcast starting January 18.</p><p> Is that any reason to split the DVD release up?
