Ithought he had one-hit wonder written all over him, but that's what I get for confusing the actorwith the role.</p><p> Cutthroat Island ($19.99 versus $9.98 on regular DVD; Lionsgate) -- If you're gonna introduce some cheaply priced BluRays, it's no surprise they'd start with a notorious flop like this pirate movie starring Geena Davis.</p><p> She's perfectly convincing as a swashbuckler but the film is bloated,as advertised.</p><p> And even at $20, it's still twice as much as the regular DVD.</p><p> Wild Pacific ($34.99 versus $29.98 on regular DVD; BBC/2 Entertain) -- Nature documentaries have become so routinely stunning that something as simply gorgeous as Wild Pacific barely makes a stir.</p><p> But it looks smashing on your TV and for a change the BluRay isn't wildly more expensive -- only $3 more on sale at Amazon.</p><p> Big Trouble In Little China ($29.99 versus $9.98 on regular DVD; Fox) -- A big flop a la Cutthroat Island but this supernatural spin on Escape From New York has a lot more character and a lot more fans.</p><p> Too bad John Carpenter's cultish flick costs three times as much on DVD (and twotimes as much even on sale) as the regular disc.</p><p> This is the sort of spectacle that deserves top quality.</p><p> My Cousin Vinny ($34.98 versus $14.98 on regular DVD) -- Marisa Tomei's Oscar-winning role (and no, it wasn't a mistake; she deserved it and has proved it since with fine turns in The Wrestler and elsewhere) in this amiable comedy that has grown in stature among the average fan over theyears.
