The savvy reader’s favorite website BookFilter.com chooses the best theater book of the month just for Broadway Direct.</p><p> DEATH EX MACHINA By Gary Corby The fifth entry in an amusing, critically acclaimed series set in Ancient Greece, “Death Ex Machina” takes place around an arts festival celebrating Dionysus.</p><p> A new play by Sophocles isbeing mounted but rehearsals are marred by a series of accidents that spooked cast membersconsider the work of a ghost.</p><p> Our proto-detective Nicolaos (called an “inquiry agent”) is put onthe job.</p><p> He arranges a very public exorcism ritual…which is promptly followed by the mysteriousdeath of an actor.</p><p> Oops.</p><p> Now Nico and his wife — the priestess Diotima — must ferret out thereal villain.</p><p> Australian author Gary Corby is praised for his rare skills: he combines historicaccuracy with a “fair” mystery (that is, readers can work out the solution themselves given theclues) and to top it off, it’s witty!
