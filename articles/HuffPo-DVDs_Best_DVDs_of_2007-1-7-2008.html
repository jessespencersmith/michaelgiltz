So here's a rundown of my favorite DVDs of 2007.</p><p> My guiding principle is to highlightDVDs that either introduced me to a movie or TV show I might have otherwise missed (like InBetween Days ) or a DVD whose extras deepened my appreciation of the film.</p><p> What I don't want to do is just list the DVD releases of the best films of the year.</p><p> So Ratatouille is one of my favorites of 2007 but since I didn't really explore the DVD extras and it certainly didn't introduce me to the film, it's not listed here.</p><p> Look for it on my favorite movies of the year list, coming soon (as soon as I can go see There Will Be Blood and work my way through about 20 more films, that is).</p><p> Enjoy! MGM) - An iconic film, this edition benefits from director Steven Soderbergh, who's got a second career interviewing directors for DVD commentary tracks.</p><p> Pairing him with the witty and quick-thinking director Mike Nicholspays off just as you'd hope.</p><p> THE MUPPET SHOW: THE COMPLETE SECOND SEASON ($39.99; Warner Bros.) - Finally a gross miscarriage of justice is reversed.</p><p> Warner Bros. previously released boxed sets of Kubrick's films that they insisted matched his meticulous desires when it came to presentation by showing cropped versions geared towards TV and home viewing.</p><p> But sometimes directors are idiots.</p><p> Here they've finally given in and presented the original theatrical versions in beautiful prints with useful extras.You get 2001: A Space Odyssey, A Clockwork Orange, Full Metal Jacket, The Shining, and Eyes Wide Shut.</p><p> What amazes the most is how each and every one of them continues to improve on repeated viewing and with the passing of time.</p><p> CINEMA 16: EUROPEAN SHORT FILMS ($29.98; Warp Films) - A collection of shorts by rising and top-named directors like Ridley Scott, Christopher Nolan (is he European?), Lars Von Trier and my personal favorite Roy Andersson.</p><p> Quite simply, before DVDs these shorts would besimply unavailable even to hardcore cineastes.</p><p> For all intents and purposes, they wouldn't existbecause if a work of art can't be seen and appreciated, how can it be said to survive? BATTLESHIP POTEMKIN ($29.95; Kino) - A beautifully restored new edition of Eisenstein's masterpiece, a film so vital and electric it might have been made tomorrow instead of in the silent era.</p><p> I was torn between choosing this and I Am Cuba: The Ultimate Edition ($44.95), Milestone's beautifully packaged set of that fever dream of a cinematic postcard.</p><p> Both are essential for moviebuffs.</p><p> TWIN PEAKS DEFINITIVE GOLD BOX EDITION ($99.99; Paramount) - Finally, the entire series available in one boxed set (including the mysteriously disappearing two hour pilot which wasn't included in the previous Season One set).</p><p> Loaded with enough extras to sate even the mostfervent fan of the show.</p><p> Now you can just stop watching once the killer of Laura Palmer is revealedand pretend it's one of the greatest miniseries in TV history instead of a brilliant series that lost itsway.</p><p> BOB DYLAN - Two great musical DVDs about Bob: Don't Look Back 1965 Tour Deluxe Edition ($49.95; Docurama ) included even more footage from that electric moment in his career.</p><p> And Bob Dylan: The Other Side Of The Mirror ($19.98; Sony) simply and perfectly shows Dylan's explosive growth as an artist and a star via three years of performances at the Newport Folk Festival.</p><p> I alsoenjoyed Amazing Journey: The Story of the Who ($29.98; Sony) - A tremendously entertaining, adult film by Steven Spielberg, who was bedeviled by the lure of TV and theatrical re- releases into endlessly fiddling with this classic.</p><p> Here, he finally does it justice by offering theoriginal theatrical release along with every other version and a new final, final edition.</p><p> THE MAN FROM U.N.C.L.E. - THE COMPLETE SERIES ($249.99; Warner Bros.) - An iconic TV series that had passed me by got the deluxe treatment on DVD, which meant I got to see this show for the first time.</p><p> A silly spy series that got sillier with the passage of time, it's atouchstone for people that grew up with it and fun for those who didn't.</p><p> Best of all, lavish extraswere clear-eyed enough to discuss the show's artistic stumbles with a fan's loving exactitude.</p><p> Andsometimes, just getting the show on DVD at all is reward enough.</p><p> That's certainly the case withSaturday Night Live Complete Second Season ($69.98;
