Right now? Once upon a time, it was Ingrid Bergman who certainly at the time of Casablanca was the most bewitching, vulnerable, enigmatic and beautiful woman in the world.</p><p> You'd gladly throw away a chance to win World War II if it meant spending the rest of your life with her. (Bogey is a better man than me.) The new collection Ingrid Bergman in Sweden captures this talent before she came to Hollywood.</p><p> A Woman's Face plays a bit like a Douglas Sirk film.</p><p> June Night is intriguing for casting Bergman in the role of a woman embroiled in an infamous scandal, a role she would unfortunately play in real life.</p><p> But Intermezzo is the keeper here; I prefer this version of the tale of a classical pianist to her Hollywood remake if only because we get Gosta Ekman instead of the pallid Leslie Howard.</p><p> All threefilms are presented with care by Kino in fine prints.</p><p> SOPHIA LOREN AWARD COLLECTION ($49.95; Lorber Films) -- Before Bergman perhaps the most beautiful woman in the world was another Swede, Greta Garbo.</p><p> After her came many others.Sophia Loren is certainly a beauty.</p><p> But I'd call her the sexiest woman in the world at her peak, which is avery different title.</p><p> In general, models seem more sexy than beautiful, perhaps because actresses get toshow a whole range of emotions.</p><p> We see Bergman weep and be strong, plead and beguile, flirt and storm,whereas most supermodels only get to pout.</p><p> Loren is a great figure of Italian cinema, but she's more of apouter than an actress.</p><p> A new collection shows her at her absolute peak in Yesterday, Today andTomorrow, which also includes a fine documentary about director Vittorio De Sica.</p><p> Her other work quickly became more like potboilers, including the melodrama Sunflower , though the sex farce Marriage, Italian Style cemented Loren and Italy itself forever in the mind of Americans as a va-va- voom sort of country.</p><p> And who claims the title today as the most beautiful woman in the world? Angelina Jolie? January Jones (who already has the perfect name for a Bond girl)? Tell me, who is your pick? "You Always Hurt the One You Love." OK, so NOT a date movie.</p><p> Just agreat one.</p><p> THE UNKNOWN WAR: WWII AND THE EPIC BATTLES OF THE RUSSIAN FRONT ($39.97; Shout) -- During World War II, some 10 million soldiers in the Soviet Union died during battle alone (another 10-14 million civilians died as a result of war).</p><p> That's virtually double the number of Germanswho died in battle and dwarfs the roughly 400,000 Americans and a similar number of Brits who also perished in battle.</p><p> In short, the USSR literally bled to defeat Hitler and that country deserves the lion's share of credit when it comes to personal sacrifice.</p><p> This landmark documentary hosted by Burt Lancasterwas made in 1978 (during the height of the Cold War) to correct how little the West knew about Russia'ssacrifice, but was pulled from the air after the Soviet Union invaded Afghanistan.</p><p> Now it can be seen MOST POPULAR ON HUFFPOST 1 of 2 'Web Cam 101 For Seniors' Turns Oregon Oldsters Into YouTube Celebrities Scarlett Johansson Nude Photos Prompt Investigation SHOCK VIDEO: Debate Audience Says Uninsured Patient Should Be Left To Die Celestrellas: Angola named Miss Universe 2011 - show recap (PHOTOS & VIDEOS) AOL TV: Jon Cryer, Ashton Kutcher & Angus T.</p><p> Jones Talk 'Two and a Half Men' Season 9 (VIDEO) Ten Signs Your Marriage is Headed for Divorce Mark Blankenship True Blood Sucker Punch: Season 4, Ep. 12 Engadget: Windows 8 for tablets hands-on preview (video) AOL Jobs: Spelling Errors Send Red Flags To Employers DON'T MISS HUFFPOST BLOGGERS 1 of 5 Kumi Naidoo Donâ€™t Make a Wave: Greenpeace at 40 Candy Spelling iPads and Social Media in the Classroom? Not often.</p><p> Either a conceit (especially of the twisty TwilightZone kind) is perfect at 25 minutes or it can be expanded to feature length.</p><p> A 50 minute episode almost always feels padded or on rare occasions rushed; Kino) -- I remain mind-boggled by the cinematic treasures now casually offered up to movie fans at their leisure.</p><p> These historic shorts from France are the sort of rarity you might see once or twice in a lifetime if you lived in a major city and caught a super-rare screening of these works at say MoMa in New York.</p><p> Now it's out there for any and allto sample.</p><p> If Quentin Tarantino was the result of B movies being available on VHS tapes at Blockbuster,we can only imagine how film directors of the future will be impacted by the wealth of cinema now therefor the viewing.</p><p> This three-DVD set includes three pioneers.</p><p> Emile Cohl experimented with animationand was a clear precursor to the playfulness of Winsor McCay on film.</p><p> Jean Durand was an early exampleof a French director besotted with American genres, often delivering his own spin on the Western.</p><p> AndJacques Feyder aspired to the sophisticated heights of Lubitsch.</p><p> THE WILD THORNBERRYS SEASON 1 ($29.93; Shout) -- I think I'd go crazy if I had kids and they wanted to see their favorite TV shows on DVD.</p><p> Again and again, episodes get doled out on DVD 4 or 6 at a time, forcing parents to buy two or three expensive sets just to get an entire season.</p><p> This goofy entertaining show about Eliza and her family traveling the globe on adventures debuted in 1998.</p><p> Now we finally get the entire first season in one slim, reasonably priced package.</p><p> Every show should get thistreatment and it shouldn't take a decade to happen.</p><p> If you don't know the show, it's perfect for kids witha yen for nature, the outdoors or just silly fun.</p><p> BANANAS ($29.99;
