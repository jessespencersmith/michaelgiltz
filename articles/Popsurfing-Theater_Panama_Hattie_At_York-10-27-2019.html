PLAYHOUSE AT HUNTER COLLEGE Sometimes a show isn't very good.</p><p> Sometimes the performances are mostly...enthusiastic.</p><p> Yet in the right context and with the right crowd,a fine time can be had.</p><p> First up: Panama Hattie, a (much) lesser Cole Porter vehicle for Ethel Merman from 1940.</p><p> It's the final production of the York Theatre's season devoted to "mufti" performances of three Porter shows.</p><p> That is,staged readings presented casually in street clothes so you can get asense of what rarely seen shows are actually like.</p><p> It's been a good season.</p><p> The musical Fifty Million Frenchmen has a superior score and clutch of songs they did right by.</p><p> With a tightened (or new) book, you could put it on commercially.</p><p> The revue Declineand Fall... is a signal work from the 1960s well worth revisiting.</p><p> It could and perhaps should have extended -- no changes needed.</p><p> And now this military-loving, flag-waving bit of nonsense.</p><p> It's not good and thus it's precisely the sort of show that should be presented in this way.</p><p> The movie bears little resemblance to the play and ditchedmost of the songs.</p><p> The original score has never been recorded.</p><p> The stage version will never be revived commercially, so you won't see it any other way So the York deserves plaudits for putting on this evening, giving people a That's precisely how it seemed to me.</p><p> The staging is so faithful andtraditional you'll think you've slipped back in time.</p><p> The large ensemble is filled with very enthusiastic performers and a few ringers in the lead roles.</p><p> The set is spot-on and could have beenlifted from Topsy-Turvy.</p><p> The one shining exception is the large and very impressive orchestra, which is conducted by Albert Bergeret, the company's founder, artistic director and general manager.</p><p> The musicianship would do Broadway proud.</p><p> That aside, if you werestationed in India when the British Empire was in full force and thelocals staged G&S, the vibe of this Iolanthe is exactly what you'd expect to see.</p><p> You get the strong feeling the audience is revisiting old friends, by which I mean G&S, Iolanthe and the actors on stage, all of whom are undoubtedly familiar to them over the years.</p><p> That camaraderie andsense of people picking out their favorites extends to the main role ofthe Lord Chancellor, played by crowd favorite James Mills.</p><p> He's playedevery role possible and when not on stage he's stage managing, handling tech and so on.</p><p> Mills delivered up "Love, unrequited, robs me of my rest" with aplomb...all four times.</p><p> Just to prove myignorance, I only knew the song from Mandy Patinkin's solo debut,with not a clue where it came from.</p><p> Lifting up proceedings appreciably was Angela Christine Smith as the Fairy Queen and David Macaluso as the romantic lead Strephon.</p><p> Theyexcelled in both singing and acting, a rarity.</p><p> But no cavils.</p><p> Is it anysurprise the Executive Director David Wannen both intro'd the show and popped into a small role as the strapping Private Willis?
