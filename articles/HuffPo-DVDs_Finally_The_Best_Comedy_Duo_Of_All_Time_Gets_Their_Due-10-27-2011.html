RHI) -- All my life, I've been told that Laurel & Hardy were the greatest comedy pairing in movie history.</p><p> But I've had to take it on faith, since the only easily available movies were from the late period of their career while the dozens of sound and silent shorts from the L&H prime remained mostly unseen.</p><p> Cruelly, the UK released a lavish boxedset a DECADE ago, but fans in the US remained in a fine mess.</p><p> Until now.</p><p> This compact boxed set contains more than 50 sound shorts and features from their peak years working for Hal Roach.</p><p> The sound and picture quality is quite good and the extras are almost overwhelming -- you don't just get theEnglish language shorts, you also get multiple versions of some shorts that were shot simultaneously inother languages like French and Spanish, versions that were often substantially longer to boot.</p><p> Plus they have the usual extras, like appreciations from other artists like Jerry Lewis and Dick Van Dyke, as well as audio tracks by scholars.</p><p> But above all you have the shorts and films.</p><p> I started to watch themchronologically to see how L&H adapted to sound.</p><p> But then I couldn't help myself and jumped ahead to classics like "Busy Bodies" (one of Stan Laurel's favorites -- and he was the brains of the bunch).</p><p> It's unquestionably one of the most important releases of the year and certainly the funniest.</p><p> It's a pity wedon't get the silent shorts as well (any fan who wants this set surely wants those included as well).</p><p> But at $100 for ten discs (and much cheaper on sale), this is a great deal and no one should hesitate.</p><p> Nonetheless, I do have complaints, mainly about the awful, awful packaging.</p><p> Go online to any websiteand you'll hear fans moan bitterly over "cardboard sleeves." And yet DVD makers continue to use them.Why? Are they that much cheaper than other options? The set is nicely compact, essentially the same dimensions as a regular DVD (but thicker), which I love because it fits right alongside your regular collection.</p><p> Bulky and odd-shaped boxed sets are a pain.</p><p> That's about all they got right.</p><p> It lookshandsome, with each thick, cardboard page filled with glossy images and stills.</p><p> But those thick cardboard pages are where the DVDs are hidden and you must slide them out awkwardly every time you want to watch a movie.</p><p> It's impossible to do without smudging the DVD plus they get easily scratched when youslide them in and out, no matter how careful you are.</p><p> The ONLY way to safely and easily store DVDs is with the spindle so the DVD can snap into place and be easily removed by the edges.</p><p> Any sort of sleeve -- especially ones as tight and awkward as these -- is just asking for trouble.</p><p> I still strongly recommend thisset.</p><p> The quality of the films and shorts inside and the reasonable pricing make it a gem.</p><p> I'm going to besavoring it for weeks to come.</p><p> Stan's weepy, deadpan face, Ollie's frustrated stares right into the camera (which kill me every time), their faultless timing -- it gets better and better every time.</p><p> In a month or two, I'll probably agree with the experts that L&H are the greatest comedy duo of all time.</p><p> WINNIE THE POOH ($39.99 BluRay combo or $29.99 DVD;
