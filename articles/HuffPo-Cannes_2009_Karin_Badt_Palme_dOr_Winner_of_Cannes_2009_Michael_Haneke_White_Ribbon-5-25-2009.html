The original idea was a children's choir, who want to make absolute principles concrete, and those who do not live up to them.</p><p> Of course, this is also a period piece: we looked at the photos that we used to determine costumes, sets, even haircuts.</p><p> I wanted to describe the atmosphere of the eve of the world war.</p><p> There are countless films that deal with the Nazi period, but not the pre-period, which is why I wanted to make this film, that presents the pre-conditions.</p><p> It is always the private questions that are most important.</p><p> Of course, my concerns are different when writing or shooting.When writing the script, I am concerned with sociological and philosophical issues.</p><p> On the set, youare asking if this actor is wearing the right tie, if the sound is ok.</p><p> The details are important.</p><p> This is where the film director steps in: otherwise sociologists would be making film.</p><p> Why are your films always so disturbing? To function, art has to rub salt in the wounds.</p><p> What interests me when I read a book or a movie are works that make me uneasy, that make me think of new problems, instead of those that reassure me.</p><p> The films that I retain are those that disturb me.</p><p> I often say that if my entire corpus were to be given one title, the title would be Civil War.</p><p> What is your film saying about Christianity? I wanted to depict the children who in their adult life would play a role in the fascist period, and these people were determined by Protestantism.</p><p> If made in Italy, of course it would be a different influence.</p><p> You do not have to look very far to see a comparison to things going on today.</p><p> Islam isthe same: obsessed with a certain idea, a certain vision of religion, which has nothing to do withreal religion.</p><p> Queen And Anne Hathaway's White Queen: FIRST LOOK (PHOTOS) The Tim Burton...</p><p> Mark Sanford: Where Is He?
