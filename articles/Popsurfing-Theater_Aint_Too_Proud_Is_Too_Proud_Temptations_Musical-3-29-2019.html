FRIDAY, MARCH 29, 2019 THEATER: A Too-Proud "Ain't Too Proud" AIN'T TOO PROUD ** out of **** IMPERIAL THEATRE OK, enough bitching and moaning about jukebox musicals and revues.</p><p> They can be great ( Jersey Boys, Ain't Misbehavin', Side By Side By Sondheim ), they can be good ( Beautiful ) and as we've learned time and time again, they can be terrible.</p><p> Instead, here's a cautionary note about making bio-musicals when the people involved are still alive.</p><p> I know, I know.</p><p> I just listed some showsabout people who are still alive and those came off just fine.</p><p> But moreoften you end up with Motown , a sanitized and duller-than-dull waste of talent.</p><p> Or you get The Otis Williams Story, aka Ain't Too Proud, a celebration of one of the greatest groups in music history via a musicalso detailed, so exhaustive, so dreadfully comprehensive that by the endyou'll swear you've seen the hiring and firing of every one of the twodozen men who've been in The Temptations, including their exit interviews with HR.</p><p> What a shame, since you'll find so much talent on stage singing so many great songs...albeit songs that are almost never performed fromstart to finish.</p><p> It's like spinning a radio dial.</p><p> Time and time again you hear a song you'd love to listen to but the damn dial keeps spinning.</p><p> The Temptations and Motown deserve much better and a few decadesfrom now someone will do it right.</p><p> The story is sadly unsurprising.</p><p> A rough life on the streets, an escape via music, fame and fortune thanks to innate talent and the star- making machinery of Motown...and then the fallout from drugs andpressure and abusive childhoods that didn't prepare some of thesemen for any life, much less a successful one.</p><p> More than most pop groups (but not so uncommon in long-running gospel and world music acts like the Blind Boys Of Alabama andMICHAEL GILTZ AT WORK Michael Giltz is a freelance writer based way-too-long introduction for a band's induction into the Rock And Roll Hall of Fame.</p><p> Thank God the snatches of music keep you from losing faith.</p><p> The exceptional playwright Dominique Morisseau wrote the book and I'm assuming she never had the creative control needed to dramatizethis story.</p><p> Indeed, almost none of this story is dramatized.</p><p> Eightypercent of the time, original Temp Otis Williams (and the author ofthe book the show is based on) simply steps to the front of the stage and narrates the action.</p><p> It's deadly dull and you can imagine Morisseau's heart sinking as this became the dominant way of movingthe show forward.</p><p> But there's always the music, right? Barely.</p><p> As with the musicalMotown , song after song is snipped or tossed into a medley or interrupted or simply put in an absurd context that denudes it of all impact.</p><p> The first act is almost over before a single song is performed from start to finish.</p><p> The choreography by Sergio Trujillo combines theclassic moves of the band with the demands of the show nicelyenough.</p><p> But they strike the classic Temptations pose (hands behind the back at the end of a song) so many times that it loses all power.</p><p> As with the desire to shove in pieces of so many tunes, more is mostdefinitely less.</p><p> Direct Des McAnuff is definitely off his game, with the constant nods to a cinematic flow leaving any emotion far behind.</p><p> In one scene Otis meets a girl and two seconds later they're married and he's on theroad.</p><p> It's hard to feel any emotional investment in the mismatchedcouple especially since the first chance we get to catch our breath andsee them together again is when they're breaking up.</p><p> Ditto the► June (3) ► May (9) ► April (10) ▼ March (5) The 44th Annual IRA Awards -- "Capernaum" Triumphs...</p><p> THEATER: A Too-Proud "Ain't Too Proud" THEATER: "Ain't No Mo'" Takes Off With Talent and ...</p><p> THEATER: "Kate, Do I Have Your Consent To Ask For ...</p><p> THEATER: "White Noise" Yearns To Provoke ► February (1) ► January (5) ► 2018 (38) ► 2017 (6) ► 2016 (2) ► 2015 (20) ► 2014 (2) ► 2013 (5) ► 2012 (17) ► 2011 (15) ► 2010 (10) ► 2009 (43) ► 2008 (86) ► 2007 (781) ► 2006 (2412) ► 2005 (5)addiction travails of singer Paul Williams.</p><p> In one scene we suddenly see Otis referencing Paul as drinking heavily (it's news to us).</p><p> About sixty seconds later he's fall-down drunk on-stage and then out of the group.</p><p> Weirdly, a free-basing scene is played partially for laughs. Act two is so chock full of funerals and goodbyes it's almost satirical and has all the dramatic impact of CliffNotes.</p><p> We experience virtually nothing in the entire show: we're simply told about it.</p><p> Countless otherchoices feel wrong, such as adding women to the lineup of TheTemptations at the grand finale, doing the steps alongside the guys.</p><p> I get it, the women have secondary roles in this story and it's a shamenot to showcase them whenever possible.</p><p> But The Temptations are aband of brothers and their cultural importance is bound up in thatimage of proud black men sharing a stage.</p><p> It makes no more sense tothrow women into the mix than it would to have men shimmyalongside the Supremes at the finale of their story.</p><p> The worst part of Ain't Too Proud may be how good the cast is that they assembled and then wasted.</p><p> Derrick Baskin has the role of alifetime as Otis and stays appropriately unassuming despite spendingmost of the show in the spotlight.</p><p> Ephraim Sykes and Saint Aubyn arethe ferocious lead singers David Ruffin and Dennis Edwards.</p><p> Their scene at a concert where Sykes as Ruffin jumps up onstage to reclaim the spotlight is hilarious and just one example of how good theirvoices are.</p><p> They both nod to the great singers they're playing withoutfalling into mimicry.</p><p> Melvin Franklin is an amiable presence incharacter and a key voice in performance as the bass singer Melvin Franklin.</p><p> And the sexy, silky-voiced Jeremy Pope as the argumentative Eddie Kendricks just proves what was obvious with his Broadwaydebut a few months ago in Choir Boy : he's a star.</p><p> My guest is a sharp-eared musical expert and Broadway fan, but themusic in the show was so muffled throughout he wondered how muchwas canned.</p><p> Similarly, I felt the sound design by Steve CanyonKennedy was off because to me at time the vocalists seemed faint andfar away, overwhelmed by the music -- canned or not.</p><p> But no! At the last second, a curtain is raised and an 18-strong orchestra is revealed, sounding fuller and richer than they have all night.</p><p> The stage isjammed with the cast, the audience explodes with pleasure and for aminute or two as they trade off vocals on "Cloud Nine," that's exactlywhere we are.Why did they wait till the very end to take us there? NOTE TO THE FUTURE: When you tackle the Temptations story again, just tell one slice.</p><p> Me, I'd focus in on their transition from Motown's biggest male group (sorry, Otis; no one was bigger or moreimportant than the Supremes) to one with a social consciousness.</p><p> You'll have the battle royale between Smokey Robinson and Norman Whitfield.</p><p> You'll have the band pulled kicking and screaming intoWhitfield's psychedelic era.</p><p> That producer kept them relevant and coolfor generations to come when they were just about to becomeyesterday's news, but they hated it, at first.</p><p> Sure, the show devotes a good ten minutes to this, but it's wrapped up in a bland pro forma "scene where we hear about the assassination of MLK" and "scenewith footage from Vietnam," which does the show and the story no favor.</p><p> You'd find a lot more drama in that behind the scenes fight than in watching them quite naturally be devastated over the death of Dr.King.</p><p> Despite what amounts to a cameo for Smokey, he was really thesixth Temptation.</p><p> The band was his baby almost as much as his own group the Miracles.</p><p> Watching them evolve from "My Girl" to epics like "Papa Was A Rolling Stone" and hard-hitting tunes like "MessageFrom A Black Man," "Slave" and "You Make Your Own Heaven andHell Right Here On Earth" would be thrilling.</p><p> You'd go from sweet classics like "Since I Lost My Baby" and "The Way You Do The Things You Do" to genuinely funky 70s gems like the above.</p><p> You'd get thebitter and the sweet.</p><p> And you'd play the songs from start to finish.Need to brush up on their psychedelic era?
