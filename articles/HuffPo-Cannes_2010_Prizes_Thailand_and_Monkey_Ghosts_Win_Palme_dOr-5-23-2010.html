DAY TWELVEUN HOMME QUI CRIE/A SCREAMING MAN ** out of **** -- It's curious.</p><p> Africa regularly produces brilliant authors, playwrights, artists and of course musicians.</p><p> But its film industry has been hobbled and never flourished artistically.</p><p> Perhaps the micro-budget movement will enable an artist to appear.</p><p> But up til now, African cinema has focused on noble and important issues delivered with stilted acting and rudimentary technical accomplishment.</p><p> A Screaming Man is a step forward, with a solid plot about a man in Chad whose life revolves around his job as a pool attendant.</p><p> He's called Champ by everyone because he won a major swimming title in his youth, he was the first pool attendant in all of Chad and he's been working at a major hotel for decades.</p><p> Now his son works alongside him, but newmanagement demotes him to gate attendant and he's devastated.</p><p> Meanwhile, the country is collapsing as rebels encroach on the government in power.</p><p> The acting is better than average for the most part and the story holds your interest.</p><p> It's something to build on but not on a world class level.</p><p> Yet.</p><p> But Africa's time will come.</p><p> OUTRAGE * out of **** -- Takeshi Kitano hasn't made a true yakuza film in about a decade.</p><p> I'd love to report that the time away from the genre has refreshed
