sayings, prayers, psalms,histories and biography, adjudicated at various times by early Catholic Church leaders and otherChristian faiths, controversially translated into English (a heresy punishable by death at one point) andthen gloriously translated into English for the King James Version in 1611 after some seven years ofwork.</p><p> That version was completed by dozens of scholars, surely making it the greatest work of literatureever produced by committee.</p><p> Some faiths teach that a particular version of the Bible is the literal word ofGod, but whatever your faith (or lack of it), the Bible in its many forms contains words that can and doinspire and comfort.</p><p> Grayling, an atheist, said, hey, that's a clever idea.</p><p> Why not create a new Bible to instruct and inspire that draws upon many other philosophical sources? You can of course be a humanist and a Christian, but it'stempting to call this "a Bible for the rest of us." He's modeled The Good Book on the Bible, with varioussections like Genesis, Wisdom, Parables and so on.</p><p> The text comes from thousands of sources written byhundreds of the world's greatest thinkers: Aristotle, Bacon, Chaucer, Confucius, Darwin, Euripides,Goethe, Hafiz, Jefferson, Laozi, Locke, Montaigne, Ovid, Rimbaud, Rumi, Spinoza, Swift, Voltaire andWalpole are among the many greats he draws upon.</p><p> Here's a brief chat unrelated to this book in whichGrayling talks about his life and philosophy in general.</p><p> Even the layout is similar: short chapters, with two columns on each page and lines written in verse butwith an eye to poetry.</p><p> You could of course read it cover to cover, but it's probably best approached bydipping in here and there, as I've done.
