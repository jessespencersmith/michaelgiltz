DVDs: Lawrence of Arabia Still Dazzles With Intelligence LAWRENCE OF ARABIA 50TH ANNIVERSARY COLLECTOR'S EDITION ($99.95 BluRay boxed set or $26.99 BluRay; Sony) -- It was two in themorning and I just wanted to check outthe picture and sound quality of this newrestoration.</p><p> Almost against my will, I wasmesmerized once again by one of themost intelligent, complex and fascinatingdepictions of an historical figure on film.It helps when that historical figure is sucha rich and contradictory fellow asLawrence of Arabia and helps even morewhen he is captured in lightning flashes ofwit and anger and equal parts of self- delusion and self-awareness by Peter O'Toole in one of the great film debuts.</p><p> The filmitself remains one of the champs and this restoration is a stunner, an immediate additionto the short list of "demo" BluRays, those titles you pull out when you want to show offyour system.</p><p> The standard Bluray release has two discs while the deluxe set has even moreextras and a soundtrack CD.</p><p> It's the film itself that demands the most attention but theextras are strong.</p><p> But this elaborate boxed set -- it's handsome and grand, of course, witha hardback book filled with striking photography and a "still" from the film as well as allfour discs of content and score.</p><p> But again I ask, does anyone who designs these thingsever actually take them home? If the film Lawrence of Arabia is the center of your life, Iguess you'll be happy to have a boxed set so bulky it won't fit onto most bookcases withoutjutting out.</p><p> You might dominate a coffee table or build a new table specifically to show itoff.</p><p> Even taking the hardback book out doesn't help -- that, too, on its own sticks out over the lip of my rather deep library shelves.</p><p> It's so big and heavy it's almost designed not to fit anywhere practical.</p><p> What's the point?
