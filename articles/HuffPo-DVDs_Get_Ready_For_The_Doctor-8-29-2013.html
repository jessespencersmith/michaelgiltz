Now America has finally caught up with the decades-long career of the TARDIS-traveling Time Lord.</p><p> The budgets are bigger, many of his adventuressuspiciously occur on Earth but the Doctor is rightfully celebrated here as the iconicTV figure he's been in the UK for so long.</p><p> And none too soon: a lot is happening in theworld of the Doctor.</p><p> There's a 50th anniversary special taking place in November withMay 13, 2014 This is the print preview: Back to normal view Â» Posted: 08/29/2013 12:16 amJohn Hurt portraying some incarnation of the Doctor alongside current lead Matt Smith.</p><p> Then Smith says goodbye during the Christmas special with Peter Capaldi taking over for the next batch of shows.</p><p> Whew.</p><p> If you do need catching up, there's no better place to start then one of the Doctors Revisited sets.</p><p> This one covers four doctors in nice retrospective fashion (including my first Doctor, Tom Baker) along with a complete adventure for each of them.</p><p> At just $40 list, that's an absolute bargain for newbies since just one complete adventure -- such as Jon Pertwee's The Green Death -- usually runs $35 at list.</p><p> Granted, this edition comes loaded with all sorts of extras, an almost overwhelming amount of detail and bonus videos literally too numerous to mention.</p><p> Just to confuse you, the Doctor is finally coming out on BluRay, beginning with Pertwee's Spearhead From Space.</p><p> That makes sense since this classic tale (also included on the Doctor Revisited omnibus DVD) was the first one shot in color.</p><p> Why is this confusing? Because they're only charging $30 list for the BluRay compared to $35 for DVD.</p><p> Okay, let me say this again.</p><p> I LOVE the Doctor.</p><p> But I don't care how many extras they cram onto the discs, these sets are wildly overpriced and woefully skimpy on actualshows.</p><p> Are they really going to release 50 years of episodes on BluRay, withindividual but expensive releases for every single adventure? (An adventure in the olddays usually lasted in a three or four episode arc.) This is soaking of loyal fans in away that the Doctor himself would be ashamed to see.</p><p> Someone take a sonicscrewdriver and fix this mess.</p><p> Massive individual and definitive boxed sets for eachDoctor -- and when the limited editions sell out, less expensively packaged copies ofthe same.</p><p> Then a greatest hits set for each Doctor of their best few adventures (moreor less depending on how long they played the role).</p><p> Then INEXPENSIVE sets foreach season of each Doctor if you think there's demand.</p><p> But really complete sets foreach one should suffice. (Only Tom Baker ran for a daunting seven seasons.</p><p> The restusually ran for three or four.) The massive markup of episodes that aired decades agois not smart on a business level -- how many fans will actually bother to buy all of them -- or on a franchise level, since it keeps so many people from dipping into the history of the Doctor.</p><p> For the love of God, stop the madness.</p><p> Enough ranting.</p><p> Let's get fanboyish again.</p><p> Peter Capaldi is the next very traditional choice -- a more mature man as most Doctors were, rather than a person of color or awoman as some expected.</p><p> But he's a strong though little known actor to most.</p><p> Luckyyou.</p><p> You get to check out his earlier work in the terrific series The Thick Of It.</p><p> This scathing political comedy is the best of its kind since Yes, Minister, another British import.</p><p> The first four seasons of The West Wing are great but I'll take the biting wit on display here over the inspirational tale that is The West Wing any day.</p><p> I guess I just prefer reality to fantasy.</p><p> Sometimes.</p><p> THE WALKING DEAD COMPLETE THIRDSEASON ($79.99 BluRay; Anchor Bay) -- In three seasons, The Walking Dead has gone from guilty pleasure to very good drama togreat drama.</p><p> Look no further than the brilliantstand-alone episode "Clear," in which SheriffRick, his increasingly troubled son Carl andthe fierce Michonne go on a road trip to get weapons.</p><p> In a haunting touch involving a would-be hitchhiker, the show neatlycaptures the moral complexity of their worldwithout saying a single word.</p><p> It's a searingexample of what it costs to survive andwhether surviving is worthwhile if you've lostyour soul.</p><p> This is the heart of the series andthey explore the question with remarkablepersistence and engaging storytelling thatupsets and confounds and challenges you atevery turn.</p><p> Yes, it's gory as all get out butThe Walking Dead is serious television any fan of quality drama needs to see.</p><p> THEMANY LOVES OF DOBIE GILLIS THE COMPLETE SERIES ($139.99 DVD; Shout) - - Shout has firmly established itself as a source of great boxed sets like this -- offbeat movies and TV shows that might otherwise slip through the cracks.</p><p> It's the sort of stuffthat Rhino once rescued on CD from the music vaults.</p><p> The Many Loves Of DobieGillis is by no means a great show or a cult classic, but is genuinely odd and ground- breaking in a quiet sort of way.</p><p> And what casting.</p><p> Genuine beats must have beenhorrified to see themselves parodied so quickly on TV (the show began in 1959) butBob Denver would never have a better part.</p><p> And what casting? After Denver (who ofcourse earned eternal fame on Gilligan's Island), the first season featured never to bebeat cast-members like the luminous Tuesday Weld as his love interest and Warren Beatty as his rival. (Dobie doesn't stand a chance.) Dobie's frank desire for girls, girls, girls and Weld's just as frank desire for money, money, money feels modern, along with the show's breaking of the fourth wall.</p><p> Even better, they avoid the foolishness of having adults play high school students by rushing Dobie and his beatnik pal into theArmy and finally college in the second and third seasons.</p><p> It's never quite as good asyou want it to be but it's not nearly as mindless as many of the shows that followed inthe 1960s and 1970s.</p><p> Extras are modest, especially for a show with such acomplicated history.
