Being Dead *** Part Two: Don't Follow Me Around *** Part Three: One Minute Of Darkness ** NEW YORK FILM FESTIVAL The New York Film Festival kicks into high gear on Day Two with the German trilogy Dreileben . it contains three films -- each about 90 minutes in length -- all linked in some way to the story of an alleged killer on the loose.</p><p> You immediately think of the UK Red Riding trilogy, which looked into the corruption and decay of the Yorkshire police.</p><p> It was based on a quartet of books by David Peace.</p><p> These films are originals, each one directed by a different rising talent from Germany.</p><p> Another key difference is that the Red Riding films were first and foremost crime stories, whereas in Dreileben the man on the loose is merely a haunting presence until the final film.</p><p> Part One is called "Beats Being Dead" and it's the best of the bunch.</p><p> In it, a young man studying to be a doctor is in the somewhat isolated region of Dreileben.</p><p> He's a favorite of the top man but on the outswith the man's pretty daughter, who our hero had been dating.</p><p> That leads him unexpectedly into the arms of a young local woman, who is from a different class and indeed from a different world.</p><p> She's friends with a biker gang of sorts and their tense, uneasy but immediately sexual relationship has a nicesee-saw quality
