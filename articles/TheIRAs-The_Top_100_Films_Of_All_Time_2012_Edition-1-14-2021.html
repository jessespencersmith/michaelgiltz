Newer Post Older PostTUESDAY, MARCH 27, 2012 The IRA Top 100 Films Of All Time The IRA film group I've been a member of for 20 years gathers annually to name their top movies of the year.</p><p> You can see our top picks for 2011 here.</p><p> This year -- in conjunction with the every-decade Sight & Sound poll (due out in August) -- we made our picks for the top films of all time.</p><p> The final vote over the March 23-25 weekend was the culmination of months of debate, argument and balloting.</p><p> First we each watched a bunch of movies we hadn't seen in years or classics which slipped through the cracks.</p><p> Then we made our own personal lists of the Top 100 films of all time.</p><p> We took all those lists, combined them and came up with a master list of the 100+ films that got the most votes.</p><p> Then we broke ties and narrowed it down to exactly 100 films.</p><p> I for one had never seen 20 of them so that meant more movie watching.</p><p> Finally, on the big night we took those 100 films ten by ten and re-ranked them after discussion and debate.</p><p> So the Top 10 vote getters (movies like The Magnificent Ambersons, The Searchers and Vertigo ) were all treated as a group and ranked from #1 to #10.</p><p> Then we did the same with the next batch of ten and so on.</p><p> The result? A list none of us are truly happy with, naturally.</p><p> I bemoan the lack of animated films and just one documentary that made the cut.</p><p> Others are embarrassed by the omission of Bollywood.</p><p> And so on.</p><p> But it's a good list: watch all 100 films and you will have a good grounding in some classic cinema.</p><p> It happened for me.</p><p> I somehow had never caught director Max Ophuls.</p><p> Now The Earrings Of Madame De... is high on my personal list of the best movies of all time.</p><p> Same with the ground-breaking, revelatory Jeanne Dielman.</p><p> So debate and mock the list, but do watch the movies you've never seen before.</p><p> Enjoy!
