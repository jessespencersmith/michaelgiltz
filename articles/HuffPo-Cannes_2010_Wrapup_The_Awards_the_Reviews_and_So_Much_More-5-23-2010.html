Dad: No, honey.</p><p> Marmaduke didn't make it past the selection committee at Cannes.</p><p> BEST DISCUSSION OF CURSING IN FILM From a Hollywood Reporter Q&A with Stephen Frears about his well-received comedy Tamara Drewe.</p><p> The Hollywood Reporter: The word c--t is in the film on at least two notable occasions.</p><p> Is it a word you feel emboldens a script? Frears: You're allowed two.</p><p> I would have liked three.</p><p> If you only have two you get an '15' certificate for a comedy from the British Board of Film Classification.</p><p> If you have three, you get the next one up, an '18.' We had to decide which one of the three to remove and when I saw the cut with one taken out I didn't notice it had gone.</p><p> Which perhaps speaks to it not being necessary.</p><p> As to whether or not it emboldens a script, I don't think it makes a blind bit of difference.</p><p> It's not a word I feel necessitates debate.</p><p> BEST SHOW OF ANGER BY A FRIEND OF THE DIRECTOR In a screening of the three hour long film The Autobiography of Nicolae Ceausescu, , seated in the area reserved for people associated with the film was an elderly woman, who one imagined was either the mother of the director or, who knows, a relative of Ceausescu himself.</p><p> Invariably, people don't stay for anentire film, especially one that's so long: they may have a meeting to attend or an interview to conduct and simply wanted to get an idea of what the film was like.</p><p> But every time someone stood up to leave, this woman would gesture with fury at them for daring to go.</p><p> And the more people that left (it wasn't aparticularly well-reviewed film, I'm afraid), the angrier she would get.</p><p> Bets were placed, but unfortunately she didn't confront anyone or trip them up as they walked by.</p><p> WORST BOOK TO BRING TO A GREGG ARAKI FILM I always bring a book along to every screening.</p><p> Half an hour in line or spent seated in a theater is just another chance to do some
