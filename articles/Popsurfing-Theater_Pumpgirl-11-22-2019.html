FRIDAY, NOVEMBER 22, 2019 THEATER: "Pump Girl" Finds A Gusher At Irish Rep PUMPGIRL out of **** IRISH REPERTORY THEATRE A strong, satisfying production of a strong, satisfying play and precisely the sort of work that makes The Irish Rep so important.</p><p> Takethis and their acclaimed O'Casey Cycle earlier this season and you'vegot reason enough to support this company for years to come.</p><p> The 2006 drama Pumpgirl and Irish playwright Abbie Spallen are both new to me.</p><p> The story centers around three people we slowly realize are interconnected.</p><p> They alternate monologues addressed tothe audience, but the overlapping plots and rising tension keep the story pulsing along.</p><p> These aren't set pieces that stand alone so much as lonely voices that slowly merge into one confused cry of pain andneed.</p><p> It begins in a gas station in the North, but only just, as the program notes put it.</p><p> Pumpgirl is a tomboyish woman who works there, dealing with obnoxious regulars but waiting for visits from her pal Hammy.Our second character is a man who competes at the local motorraceway when not avoiding the burden of his wife and kids.</p><p> And thethird is a woman who dreads the moment each night her drunken husband comes home, ping-ponging up the stairs and then slipping into bed beside her, a bed with barbed wire strung right down thecenter.</p><p> The wife cheats on her husband with some random man at a local market.</p><p> Pumpgirl goes off with Hammy for quickies in his car.</p><p> And the cocky but lost lad who competes as a race car driver when not cleaningout bird shite at the local chicken farm (off the books of course, so thedole doesn't know) is both the husband and the "mate." Did hercheating subliminally stir his guilty conscience.</p><p> And Hamish Allan- Headley is an ideal Hammy, just good enough to be the handsome stud of a small town without being so good-looking you can't quite buyit.</p><p> He nails Hammy's conflicted combination of bad choices, regret,shame and yearning to maybe, maybe get something right for once.</p><p> Ilook forward to seeing all three of them again, soon.</p><p> Towards the very end, the play gets quite a bit darker.</p><p> For me, the stakes were already high enough so I resisted this, even thoughnothing was out of whack with what came before.</p><p> It's a smallreservation about Pumpgirl itself, not the sterling and loving presentation it's been given here.THEATER: "The Crucible" Is Bedlam In Every Way THEATER: "Einstein's Dreams" Too Elusive For Reali...</p><p> BOOKS: Is Finance The Root Of All Evil?
