gripping, melodramatic true story of one of the most famous circus performers of all time.</p><p> Queen Of The Airbegins with a family on hard times that reluctantly allows its little daughter to go away on tour throughout Latin America with a traveling circus.</p><p> She is abused by the owner yet rises to become one of the greatest acts in the world.</p><p> But she's not the star of this story.</p><p> Her daughter from that rape turns into a prodigy of prodigies called Leitzel, the Queen Of The Air.</p><p> Among the many unlikely events that pepper this true story: the mother iswracked with guilt that she had to ignore her children for her career but is insanely jealouswhen the little girl almost immediately outshines her.</p><p> Leitzel leaps from success tosuccess with her dazzling skill as an aerialist and is soon the top attraction at the biggestcircus in the world.</p><p> There a young nobody in the circus falls in love with her.</p><p> That teenage boy Alfredo gets to smooch with Leitzel but she's really only dallying with him.</p><p> They both marry others but Alfredo is determined to become worthy of her...andturns into a member of the Flying Codona Brothers, ultimately becoming perhaps the firstman in history to regularly perform the literally death-defying triple somersault whiletraveling at 60 miles per hour.</p><p> More than a decade later and now world famous himself,Alfredo reunites with the woman he always loved.</p><p> She loves him too and they get married.</p><p> But she loves fame ever so slightly more and is too used to flitting from lover to loverfor the jealous Alfredo's taste.</p><p> Romance, high society, passion, disdain, celebrity, money, madness, bold gambles, tragicfailures -- it's all here in a tale that you would scoff at if it weren't true.</p><p> A real life Water For Elephants is the inevitable comparison for Queen Of The Air.</p><p> Author Dean N.</p><p> Jensen took literally decades to tell this story
