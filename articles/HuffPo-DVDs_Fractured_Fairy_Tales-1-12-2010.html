Summit) -- Here's what I don't like about this film: a therapist character is too stock, too clearly there just to give our tight-lipped heroes someone to talk to in war-torn Iraq.</p><p> His behavior in the field beggars belief.</p><p> And the story overall is pretty conventional -- soldiers doing a tense, scary job and finding the adrenaline of war makes adapting tocivilian life nigh on impossible.</p><p> But what makes the movie special is the top-notch acting by the men andthe direction of Kathryn Bigelow, who is rightly the front-runner for the Oscar.</p><p> She milks more drama out of the scenes where nothing blows up than most directors could with a boatload of pyrotechnics.</p><p> One iconic shot shows a bomb disposal guy pulling up a wire and exposing a string of bombs that encircle him-- it'll take your breath away.</p><p> This is NOT an ideal movie to see at home, so if you can still go to themovie theaters now or around the Oscars, by all means do.</p><p> That sense of being "trapped" with the movie and the men doing such tense, dangerous work is hard to duplicate at home.</p><p> But then you can watch it again on DVD.</p><p> Very modest extras include a commentary track and a brief making-of docu.</p><p> Dollars todonuts they'll come out with a loaded special edition after the Oscars, so if extras are your thing you might want to wait.</p><p> HARRY POTTER -- A slew of releases as we look ahead to the final two-part movies next fall and the summer of 2011.</p><p> First, Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince ($34.99 Special Edition and $39.99 BluRay; Warner Bros. -- though on sale the BluRay is $1 cheaper than the Special Edition and $4.50more than the single disc basic edition; Whew!) In a sense, this film is treading water till the finale.</p><p> But I found it perhaps the most enjoyable since Alfonso Cuaron's Prisoner of Azkaban.</p><p> The main reason is the production design.</p><p> Hogwarts and the magical world in general look lived in and real, not like the set for a theme park ride.</p><p> That realism gives weight to the high stakes here, whether it's the soul of poor old Draco Malfoy or the heart of Dumbledore.</p><p> Extras include footage from Deathly Hallows, a look at Rowling as she laid the series to rest and the real theme park ride at Universal Orlando.</p><p> Many more extras aboundbut if extras are your thing, maybe you should wait before purchasing this film.</p><p> It's sure to be superseded by the Ultimate Edition down the road.</p><p> Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone Ultimate Edition and Chamber of Secrets Ultimate Edition ($39.92; Warner Bros.) are (presumaby) the final word in Potter DVDs until they release a boxed set with every film.</p><p> As fat as the biggest books in the series, they are truly jam-packed with both theatrical and extended versions of the films, extras and a small hardbound book of photos all packaged nicely in a slipcover case.</p><p> If you just can't get enough of these films, theUltimate Editions are for you.MORE BIG NEWS PAGES » FOLLOW HUFFINGTON POST THE MEL BROOKS COLLECTION ON BLURAY ($139.99; FOX) -- BluRay? So fancy-schmancy, Mr.</p><p> Tony-winning Putting On Airs Brooks! What? Regular DVD is not good enough for you? In fact, the low brow humor of Mel Brooks should be on VHS or even 16mm just to give the right tone of goofy, anything goes attitude to this stuff.</p><p> This set contains nine films he made for MGM and or Fox, includinghis masterpiece Young Frankenstein, the funny Blazing Saddles and High Anxiety , the unnecessary remake of To Be Or Not To Be and lots of other movies with so-so ratios of good to bad jokes, movies like Spaceballs, Robin Hood: Men In Tights, History Of The World Part I (the first R-rated movie I snuck into and the trailer for Part II is still funnier than anything else in the film), Silent Movie and his early work The Twelve Chairs .</p><p> Fine, all fine.</p><p> But they shoved it into yet another bulky, unlovable boxed set.</p><p> This is not anyone's idea of luxury; it's everyone's idea of a pain in the neck because it doesn't fit alongside your other movies and it's too easy to drive yourself crazy trying to pull out the DVDs from their little slots.</p><p> Mel, yes.</p><p> Boxed set packaging, no.</p><p> FAMILY GUY: SOMETHING SOMETHING SOMETHING DARKSIDE ($22.98 regular or $29.98 BluRay; FOX) -- Seth McFarlane must identify with George Lucas.</p><p> Both have created massively popular smash hits, spun them off into other series and yet also deal with a lot of grief from fanboys who diss them.</p><p> At least Lucas has the original Star Wars trilogy to point to with pride.</p><p> McFarlane just has FamilyGuy, which has always come way back in third place compared to The Simpsons or South Park.</p><p> So it's no surprise Family Guy would spoof Star Wars, just like Robot Chicken and so many other shows have done.</p><p> You can't really argue with those who say this take on The Empire Strikes Back isn't as fresh as their first spoof.</p><p> But maybe they'll pull it together for Return Of The Jedi.</p><p> At the very least, McFarlane and Lucas can have a beer together and commiserate over the losers like me who seem to do nothing but run them down.</p><p> MYSTERY SCIENCE THEATER 3000: VOLUME XVI ($64.99; Shout) -- Talking back to the screen during a dumb movie never gets old, as anyone who sat in a movie theater during Leap Year canattest.</p><p> This limited edition contains four more episodes never been released on DVD, including Warrior Of The Lost World and Night Of The Blood Beast and...oh, what do you care? The movies are awful, the comments are (often) funny and you get a Tom Servo toy in the bargain.</p><p> PUBLIC ENEMIES ($34.98 Special Edition or $36.98 BluRay; Universal) -- Michael Mann is too talented a director to make a completely dull movie, but he comes pretty close here.</p><p> Johnny Depp is thegangster John Dillinger, presented here as charming and decent, with Christian Bale tense and off-kilteras the G-man determined to hunt him down.</p><p> Call me old-fashioned (or maybe it's new-fashioned sinceold movies often showed criminals as lovable) but I just wasn't in the mood to see the soft side of a brutal man who killed people who got in his way.</p><p> Another problem was the talented Marion Cotillard, who perhaps wasn't quite ready to tackle her first English language role.</p><p> Her performance was always a beat or two behind, as if she had to think through the words she was saying.</p><p> Still, Stephen Lang is memorable in a small role (much better here than in his laughable cartoon of a soldier in Avatar) and it all looks smashing.</p><p> Maybe I'm just being a fuddy duddy.</p><p> Loads of extras, of course.</p><p> G-FORCE ($29.99 regular, $39.99 Special and $44.99 BluRay; Disney) -- I don't rate movies on a sliding scale.</p><p> A good movie is a good movie, whether it's a family film geared to kids or an art house filmfrom Taiwan.</p><p> But, yes, my expectations are on a sliding scale.</p><p> I wasn't expecting much from a movie about guinea pigs who double as secret agents.</p><p> How could I? But G-Force didn't even meet my modest hopes for a diverting 88 minutes.</p><p> I have no objections to a flick about talking guinea pigs on a secret mission.</p><p> Heck, I WANT to see a movie about talking guinea pigs on a secret mission.</p><p> Just not this one.</p><p> TAKING WOODSTOCK ($29.98 regular or $39.98 BluRay; Universal) -- Director Ang Lee is a square and I say that with affection for a man who has made some of my favorite films.</p><p> But he is probably completely wrong to tackle a story about the wild and wacky days surrounding the Woodstock concert.</p><p> The real Eliot Tiber had a wacky, outrageous life and his involvement in Woodstock by giving them aJames Franco On Hosting The Oscars: 'Why...</p><p> Shania Twain Engaged To Frederic Thiebaud 'The Fighter' Star Amy Adams Talks Weight...</p><p> Megan Fox In A Bikini (PHOTOS)license and seeing his folks' motel overrun with hippies was just the capper.</p><p> But here Tiber (played by Demetri Martin) is just an uptight closeted gay guy who has a mind-blowing experience that weekend but can't even find the courage to come out to his parents.</p><p> The concert is offstage, of course and thank God.You wouldn't want to see people imitating Jimi Hendrix.</p><p> Jonathan Groff is perfect casting as the angelic-looking but preternaturally calm promoter Michael Lang.</p><p> But like everyone else here, he has too little to do.</p><p> Watch the classic concert film instead.</p><p> FARSCAPE: THE COMPLETE SERIES ($149.95; A&E) -- FInally, a nod to a terrific boxed set I'm just beginning to explore.</p><p> Like many sci-fi series, Farscape has a cult following, so it's hard to suss out when a TV series is genuinely great ( Firefly ) and when it just has a loyal fanbase (I'm too afraid to say Babylon 5 or Torchwood because whatever show I mention will cause its fans to howl in anger over my idiocy).</p><p> Farscape has the bonus attraction of some of the best puppetry work Jim Henson Workshop ever did, which brings a whole new level of fandom to the table.</p><p> But in an era where bulky boxed sets for TV shows like Dawson's Creek and Ally McBeal are the standard -- making it impossible for anyone who doesn't want the series to sit on their coffee table do anything but shove it into a closet -- it's wonderful tosee a show presented compactly and with care.</p><p> All four seasons are here, along with bonus discs, allpresented in what I believe are their proper ratios (the first three seasons in standard TV box size, the fouth widescreen.) It's true that rights issues kept them from including a TV movie wrapup, but everything else is here in solid picture and sound.</p><p> And it's on sale at Amazon for $60, which is less than adollar for each of the 88 episodes.</p><p> This is exactly how TV shows should be preserved, in a relatively inexpensive manner for fans and in a way that newbies can dive in with confidence.</p><p> Well
