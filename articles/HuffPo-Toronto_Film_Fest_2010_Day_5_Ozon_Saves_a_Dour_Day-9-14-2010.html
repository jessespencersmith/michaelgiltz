Zombieland 's reason for being was a goofy, satirical sense of humor built around a how-to way of tackling zombies.</p><p> This movie has no story, really.</p><p> The kid becomes skilled at killing, but he doesn't have to learn to respect the ruthless older man Mister, nor does he have to rejectthe guy, either.</p><p> The kid Martin is an eager student at the beginning and at the end, albeit covered in more blood.</p><p> They never have a falling out, they never uncover a secret or face a new danger (not really), they never clash, they never really do much of anything except survive.</p><p> That's what they need to do tostay alive: just focus on killing and character development be damned.</p><p> Submarine ** 1/2 As is my wont, when I hear about the movies scheduled to open at Toronto or Cannes, I pick the most intriguing adaptations and read the source novel.</p><p> In this case it's the precocious novel by teen author Joe Dunthorne. (I hate him already.) His book created a distinctive central character in Oliver but was prettyover-excited about using every trick in the world to tell his tale, from lists to dictionary definitions to youname it.</p><p> Oliver (Craig Roberts) lives in Wales, lusting after the right girl and hyperactively role-playing a conversation with the girl or whisking her away or at least trying to.</p><p> There's certainly an epidemic of knowingness (I'm looking at you, Juno ) throughout pop culture.</p><p> Happily, this movie downplays that with clever production design schemes and a plan to walk out the door and show this film on the side of a building if necessary.</p><p> Oliver is self-critical and quite funny, callously taking part in the bullying of a heavy girl in school to curry favor with Jordana.</p><p> As the calculating, manipulative but really just playfuland fun Jordana, actress Yasmin Paige scores a real triumph.</p><p> Roberts has the more difficult role of trying to make simple chores seem distinctive and new.</p><p> Though the film has more tricks than GoodFellas , the movie is quite faithful to the book while avoiding the more arch moments for genuine emotion.</p><p> It ends in a lovely manner and would have been even better if it wasn't so clear when characters were being "eccentric."
