Miller brokeout with the film Trainspotting and has enjoyed a string of well- reviewed roles in the UK.</p><p> I've mostly seen him grow tremendously asan actor on shows like Dexter (a great season long arc), the charming Eli Stone and his excellent work as Sherlock Holmes on the satisfying CBS series Elementary .</p><p> But he does his best work here, making this good-not-great drama well worth seeing.</p><p> For an ink-stained wretch like myself, the topic is fascinating.</p><p> A young Murdoch (Carvel) sees there is money to be made in the newspaperbiz.</p><p> Fleet Street is crowded with competitors in 1969 but they're alloffering the same product: news that condescends to its blue collar readers.</p><p> Carvel convinces Lamb to take over the moribund Sun despite the very long odds: they'll be stuck with a chunk of employees (theones no one else wants), the competition is fierce and the clubby worldof London journalism isn't about to make it easy for the likes of anAussie upstart like Murdoch.</p><p> Well, they never made it easy for the Yorkshire man Lamb, did they?
