(And I assume that counselor couldbe reprimanded or lose their license for engaging in therapythat the APA has condemned.</p><p> Perhaps they're only licensed bythe state?) Since it ISN'T a mental health clinic, I still takeissue with CNN describing chat sessions as "group therapy."Shouldn't group therapy be reserved only for sessions led by aprofessional? Is the only licensed therapist always present atevery session? If not, it doesn't deserve this patina ofprofessional respectability -- especially since what they practicehas been condemned by every reputable medical and mentalhealth organization in the country as dangerous, wrong, andpotentially extremely damaging.They still incorrectly describe the leader Rev.</p><p> Smid [what aname!] as "rejecting homosexuality," a claim these fringegroups know they can no longer pretend to make -- even Rev.Smid doesn't claim he's now straight, just that he won't act onthose desires.</p><p> Again, it's important to note that just a fewyears ago they lied and said people could choose to be straightand that being gay was merely a choice.</p><p> Society is so farbeyond such idiocy, today even the far right doesn't trot that lieout anymore.</p><p> Even the IDs of the people who went through theprogram are poorly thought out: two young men interviewedare labeled "graduate," which again gives this sketchyorganization that is seriously condemned by the entire medicalcommunity an air of seriousness it doesn't deserve.</p><p> You can'tgraduate from a scam.All the footage used was presumably shot the first time around.But what a difference a day makes.</p><p> With the new emphasis onfacts and the overwhelming medical opinion about this fraud,they even used a better quote from Dr.</p><p> Drescher of the APAthan the one they used the first night.
