"It's the fastest release ever!" laughed one Fox Home Entertainment ex­ ecutive. "It's coming out on DVD just days after the final episode airs." The reasoning is simple: Ifl5 million people watch the season finale of the hit reality series, why wait one year or even six months to put it out on Dvm The show will be in -danger of looking like yes­ terday's news (reality shows have a half life of about 10 minutes), and you'll just have to spend money reminding peo­ ple about the show all over again. "The Simple Life" is the most dramatic exam­ ple of a growing trend for TV shows and mov­ ies.</p><p> Studios eager to take advantage of the millions they spend promoting shows and movies -not to mention dipping into the DVD pot of gold right away -are re­ leasing the sets faster and faster.</p><p> Season one of" Alias" came out just before sea­ son two debuted on ABC, and season two of "24" came out just before sea­ son three began on Fox.</p><p> This lets hardcore fans start collecting and gives casual viewers a chance to catch up (especially im­ portant on serialized shows like "24," which ex­ plains why they are lead­ ing the DVD pack, instead of stand-alone stalwarts like "Law & Order").</p><p> This desire for instant pleasure has been building for awhile.</p><p> Baseball fans get barraged with ads urging them to buy the World Series commemo­ rative DVD before the final out has been scored.</p><p> Some TV miniseries air ads for the DVD and soundtrack before viewers find out how the story ends.</p><p> And PBS was selling Ken Burns' epic "Jazz" even before the show had debuted.</p><p> Series head to DVD faster to cash in on popularity.</p><p> Some recent releases: "The Shield" Second season ended: April 1,2003 DVD release date: Jan.6,2004 "The Simple Life" Season ends: January 14,2004 OVO release date: Jan. 20,2004 "24" Second season ended: May 20,2003 DVD release date: Sept 9, 2003 "MTV' Punk'd" First season ended: May 5,2003 .OVO release date: Jan. 20,2004 "Alias" Second season ended; May 4,2003
