(He does not know that Atlanta-based Cox Cable is one the biggest opera­ tors in the U.S.) "Why would you call something that?" laughs Norton, who has just begun a 13-week run on Comedy Central with a new talk show, "The Graham Norton Ef­ fect." "That's just mean." Mean is exactly what Norton's good-natured shtick is not.</p><p> Though his talk show is filled with silly sex toys, randy Web sites (like one devoted to a man's fetish for watch­ ing women brush their teeth) and stories that would make Congress­ man Jack Ryan blush, Norton isn't mean or dirty in the Howard Stern way.</p><p> He just reacts with shocked glee over the awfully embarrassing things celebrities and au­ dience members can't wait to reveal.</p><p> A huge star in the UK, Norton's most recent show aired five days a Norton reacts with shocked glee over the embarrassing things celebrities and audience members can't wait to reveal. week (almost unheard of in Great Britain, where they have no tradition of daily taik shows like "The Tonight Show").</p><p> So filming one show a week here (it airs Thurs­ days at 10 p.m.) seems like a vacation.
