Summit) -- Emily Bronte's masterpiece Wuthering Heights has been filmed umpteen times.</p><p> But like a classic play, it's well worth revisiting again and again.</p><p> Still, most new versions aren't terribly interesting even though every generation wants to tackle it.</p><p> Not so here.</p><p> Director Andrea Arnold has been a technically fascinating talent.</p><p> Here her formidable skills have been married to a passionatetale, bringing out a richness and humanity in her work not present before.</p><p> This was one of the most acclaimed films of the year, but was all but overlooked by the general public.</p><p> Well worth checking out.</p><p> Barbra Streisand has made so few films (relatively speaking) that each one feels like an event.</p><p> And it's disappointing when they're not up to snuff.</p><p> She's been her own worst enemy over the years, showing a Beatty-like reluctance to commit and overthinking herchoices.</p><p> So I applaud everything about The Guilt Trip -- it's loosey-goosey nature, it's comedic nature, taking a chance on young talent -- yes, everything about it except themovie itself.</p><p> Sigh.</p><p> Intellectually, I resisted Silver Linings Playbook, a sentimental drama about two misfits falling in love.</p><p> Too obvious, too determined to move me, too heart-tugging.</p><p> But the excellent cast (De Niro hasn't been this restrained and good in a long time) won me over.No wonder they were all nominated for Oscars.</p><p> I'm afraid a second viewing will be oneviewing too much for this pleasant little surprise.</p><p> But for those who didn't catch it, this isa genial movie for adults.</p><p> Promised Land should have been Matt Damon's directorial debut.</p><p> But he backed out and handed the reins to director Gus Van Sant (in pure, mainstream studio mode here).</p><p> Did Damon sense the movie wouldn't quite fulfill its potential? Hardly, since he produced andco-wrote the script.</p><p> It may also be just as telling for revealing the ambition andinteresting desire of actor John Krasinski ( The Office ) who also co-wrote the script and co-stars.</p><p> Krasinski seems to be using his modest TV success to make interesting,intelligent films.</p><p> This story nominally about fracking proves to be truly about people in aninteresting, relatively nuanced manner.</p><p> Excellent cast, including Hal Holbrook as aschoolteacher and Frances McDormand as a love interest.</p><p> A big hit overseas, The Impossible is a classic type of Hollywood movie that uses a natural disaster to show beautiful movie stars overcoming the odds to survive, surprising no one in the process.</p><p> Naomi Watts is the glamorous movie star/mother, Ewan McGregor is theglamorous movie star/father and anyone complaining that a big movie about a tsunamifocuses on the wealthy white tourists simply doesn't know much about Hollywood disaster epics.PIERRE ETAIX: THE CRITERION COLLECTION ($59.99 BluRay; Criterion Collection) RICHARD III ($39.99 BluRay; Criterion Collection) -- It's been just about one year since I first even heard the name of French director/actor Pierre Etaix.</p><p> He labored in obscurity simply because his films weren't being seen. (Legal troubles.) A circus performer besottedwith silent films, he created almost all his works in the 1960s firmly in the vein of JacquesTati.</p><p> Last year, Film Forum in NYC hosted a festival of his works to sold out crowds.</p><p> They've come back again this year.</p><p> But for those who don't live nearby (or didn't catchthem on TCM recently), Criterion has produced a beautiful boxed set including 2010restorations of all his major work.</p><p> You get five feature films -- including his masterpieceLe Grand Amour -- three shorts, a documentary about his life and career by Etaix's wife and more.</p><p> He's still alive, having fled the disastrous pressures of movies after about adecade of brilliance to pursue his muse in the circus (usually, it works the other wayaround).</p><p> A unique talent.</p><p> Why I wonder did Laurence Olivier direct so few films? Ignoring a TV movie, he directed one Hollywood trifle ( The Prince And The Showgirl ), co-directed one Chekhov and directed on his own three masterpieces of Shakespeare, each one acclaimed andcommercially successful around the world.</p><p> Henry V was a World War II morale booster, Hamlet an Oscar triumph and Richard III less so, but hardly disastrously so.</p><p> Interestingly, it was
