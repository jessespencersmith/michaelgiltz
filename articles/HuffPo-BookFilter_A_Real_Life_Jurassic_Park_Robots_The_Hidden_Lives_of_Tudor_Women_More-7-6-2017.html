(Think the Cape.) An on and off affair with the not so talented Amy O'Donnell -- who is unhappily married to a hedge fund billionaire -- and a continuing inner anger at the latest darling of the comic book world make for the substance of this book.</p><p> Fischer's deep and unabiding sarcasm make for the style.</p><p> As a comic anti-hero we are a long, long way from Portnoy's psychic scream in Philip Roth's groundbreaking novel, for Fischer is much more benign.</p><p> He doesn't rant at his mother.</p><p> He doesn't see a shrink.</p><p> He sulks instead and his long sulk is very funny....</p><p> Read More.</p><p> Discover 24 New Fiction Titles Out This Week! *************** THE HIDDEN LIVES OF TUDOR WOMEN by Elizabeth Norton British historian Elizabeth Norton pulls double duty here.</p><p> She focuses on the women of the Tudor era and details what their lives were like on a day to day basis, including childbirth as a frightening ordeal often ending in death, the utter lack of education, the endless toil and more.</p><p> Why the Tudor era? Because it's the first where women left an indelible mark on the events of the day, says Norton.</p><p> Yet she also consistently contrasts the lives of women of privilege with the servant girls and wet nurses who kept them in relative comfort, realizing that class plays an equally big role in what the hidden lives of women were like....</p><p> Read More.</p><p> Discover 12 New History Titles Out This Week! ***************PAUL MCCARTNEY HEADS TO A FAIR....</p><p> Yes, the Beatles are on constant rotation at the world headquarters of BookFilter.</p><p> Which is why we know that on July 6, 1957, music fan Paul McCartney headed to a fair at St.</p><p> Peter's Church in Liverpool to check out a band called the Quarrymen. In the book, it begins when Alice goes tumbling down a rabbit hole.</p><p> In real life, it began on July 4, 1862 when professor Charles Dodgson (aka Lewis Carroll) took Lorina, Edith and Alice -- the three daughters of his friend Henry Liddell -- on a boating trip for the day.</p><p> It was Alice's birthday (she turned 10 years old) so when she asked Dodgson to tell them a story he naturally made her the star.</p><p> And when she begged him to write it down, well eventually he did.</p><p> Two marvelous new editions include The Complete Alice (both books and fully restored artwork) and The Annotated Alice (which explains all the references and puns and jokes in glorious detail).</p><p> But for heaven's sake, if you've never read it, grab an inexpensive paperback with the classic original drawings by John Tenniel, take it to the beach or on a picnic and prepare to giggle. ***************LEAP IN by Alexandra Heminsley Heminsley hit paydirt with her memoir Running Like A Girl, in which she shared the agony and inspiration behind trying to become a serious runner.</p><p> It proved a bestseller all over the world.</p><p> Can she do it again with swimming?
