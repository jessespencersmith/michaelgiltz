never-to-be-repeated success of A Streetcar Named Desire in 1947, playwright Tennessee Williams delivered a prequel of sorts with Summer and Smoke .</p><p> It told the story of how a Blanche DuBois character might come to be.</p><p> Alma (Marin Ireland) is the daughter of a preacher who yearns for artistic refinement and a superior existence, not to mention thehandsome but callow son of the doctor next door.</p><p> Their friendshipremains just that, from a childhood fancy to adult recriminations, with Alma unable to accept anything other than a "spiritual" bond while John (Nathan Darrow) enjoys more earthly pleasures with every otherwoman in sight.</p><p> Needless to say, when Alma approaches spinsterhoodand is finally ready to savor the sins of the flesh, John has reformedhimself and pairs off with a pretty young thing.</p><p> Heartbroken and bitter, Alma tosses herself at the first of what is sure to be a string of traveling salesmen.</p><p> The clanging bell of that streetcar can almost beheard in the distance.</p><p> Summer And Smoke shows Williams beginning to reshuffle his obsessions -- damaged women, simmering desire, the stultifying mores of a small town and above all self-deception -- to less and less effect.</p><p> The melodrama would be produced fitfully over the years,marking a notable artistic triumph for actress Geraldine Page butnever really finding an audience.</p><p> Williams overhauled it completely in 1962, retitling it with the precious name of The Eccentricities Of A Nightingale.</p><p> No matter.</p><p> Classic Stage Company and Transport
