SCORING lubelunes ELEVISION MAY BE the bastard stepchild of the movies-smaller pic­ ture, smaller stories , smaller stars-but it provides a unique challenge to com­ posers.</p><p> For regular series, major themes have to be instantly memorable yet not grow tiresome week after week.</p><p> For miniseries, musical ideas must stretch to fill six hours of airtime-or more.</p><p> Here are some scores that rise to the occasion.</p><p> Wild Palms (Capitol).</p><p> Like the miniseries it­ self, Ryuichi Sakamoto's flamboyant, synthe­ sizer-driven score sometimes falls flat, but it is nevertheless smart-even inspired ("Harry to Hospital")-and makes good use of a few nifty pop hits ("Can't Take My Eyes off You").</p><p> Brideshead Revisited (Chrysalis ).</p><p> Elegant and elegiac, this is clear-eyed nostalgia for the upper crust. "Orphans of the Storm" and "Brideshead Theme" are some of the high­ lights of this beautiful reverie.</p><p> Masada (Varese Sarabande ).</p><p> Jerry Gold­ smith's majestic, mournful work is the perfect match for the tragic heroism of Judean rebels who commit mass suicide rather than surren­ der to the Romans.</p><p> An essential piece.</p><p> Also impressive is Craig Safan's score for Son of the Morning Star (Intrada), featuring an excep­ tionally complex final cue, "Lirtle Big Horn." Strange interludes: Sakamoto's Palms; Frontiere's Limits.</p><p> Inspector Morse (Caroline).</p><p> Barrington Pheloung provides low­ key but memorable music for the British series fea­ turing the brusque in­ spector, as well as con­ ducting specially recorded selections from Mozart, Puccini, and others.</p><p> More crime-fighting de­ lights: Henry Mancini's Peter Gunn (RCA), which redefined hip ("Dreamsville ," "A Pro­ found Gass"); and Sher­ lock Holmes (Varese), for which Patrick Gower's marvelous score (show- casing the violin, natural­ ly) reveals the cerebral sleuth's emotional heart.</p><p> The Outer Limits (GNP Crescendo ).</p><p> Do­ minic Frontiere 's melodramatic music is the centerpiece of this lavish package, which includes the series's famous voice intro and a sound-effects library.</p><p> Also check out The Best of the Twilight Zone (Varese), which boasts such talented names as Herrmann , Steiner, and Goldsmith.</p><p> MICHAEL GILTZ
