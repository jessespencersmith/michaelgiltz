Sure, I keep up with the kids.</p><p> I listen to the new stuff.</p><p> I bop my head to the latest beats.</p><p> You'll find Conejito Malo and Dua Lipa and Moses Sumney and the woefully under- appreciated Teyana Taylor alongside old friends of mine like Rumer and Green Day and Teddy Thompson, who isn't woefully under- appreciated, at least by me.</p><p> I can hold my head high, but it's an old head.</p><p> I've followed that cliched path of hardcore music fans, from the pop music of my youth to a back flip into what came before (the Everly Brothers! MARIA MCKEE -- La Vita Nuova Oh, wait, you mean Maria McKee was never a huge star? Her band Lone Justice didn't conquer the world, say the way Talking Heads did? Well, they did and she has, as far as I'm concerned.</p><p> Whether it's her marvelous solo debut or the classic song for the ages "Life Is Sweet" or her many other strong records, Maria McKee looms large for me.</p><p> Here she is, 35 years into her recorded life, offering up 14 strong tracks, pushing her voice into new realms, baring her soul and being true to herself, as she always has.</p><p> Ten albums in and she's hitting new peaks.</p><p> Life is sweet.</p><p> GREEN DAY -- Father of All Motherfuckers That was close! Except maybe for the fact that Teddy's talent is downbeat, subtle and rueful.</p><p> I guess rueful doesn't bring dollar signs to the eyes of record label execs.</p><p> As always, his music would play beautifully on the radio, this time with some punch from horns for good measure.</p><p> It's not really a surprise he's never become a commercial juggernaut.</p><p> He'll just have to settle for literate pop offered to adults smart enough to catch on.</p><p> Dig Ron Sexsmith or Neil Finn? No, Manilow is not in best form when going big, so even albums with big bands don't quite work for him.</p><p> He really shines at the piano, quiet, controlled and focused on the lyrics.</p><p> Every once in a while, he knuckles down and does it right. 2:00 A.M.</p><p> Paradise Cafe is his nightclub masterpiece.</p><p> But he's also got Night Songs from 2014 and now Night Songs II, piano-centric albums with the usual standards but done with such care and insight it doesn't matter that he's older and his voice might waver a little.</p><p> He's older, yes, but wiser and he knows how to sing 'em.</p><p> Oh he writes the hit songs. Lopez-Nussa almost makes me like "The Windmills Of Your Mind" and the rest is a gas.</p><p> THE STRUTS -- Strange Days A peacock of a strutting rock band, built for stadiums and more, More, MORE! Great fun.</p><p> BOB DYLAN -- Rough and Rowdy Ways I am all in on late period Bob, from his dive into the Great American Songbook usually sung by Sinatra and Ella to full-on 17 minute wig- outs where his references are so amusingly of-the-moment you feel like he's daring you.</p><p> Yeah, that's right, I won the Nobel Prize for Literature and I just name-checked Indiana Jones.</p><p> You got a problem with that? standards ( Triplicate, Fallen Angel ), this one didn't deepen on repeated listens.</p><p> Still, he's got nothing left to prove but he keeps proving it.</p><p> HAROLD BUDD AND ROBIN GUTHRIE -- Another Flower / BRIAN ENO AND ROGER ENO -- Mixing Colours Brian Eno is the Zelig of popular music, creating entire genres, helping other artists like Bowie and U2 do their best and tossing off superior solo work with disarming regularity, like this ambient collaboration with his brother.</p><p> Eno worked with Harold Budd and of course that album ( The Pearl ) proved to be one of the peaks saying something.
