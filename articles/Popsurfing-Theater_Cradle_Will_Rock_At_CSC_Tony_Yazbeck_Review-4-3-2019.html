It's a landmark show, of course, whose premiere (directed by Orson Welles!) proved electrifying for how it was performed (rather than the show itself).</p><p> That story is so famous it inspired a so-so film about it byTim Robbins.</p><p> This "play in music" (a musical ain't serious enough, I guess) was the first in Broadway history to garner an original cast recording.</p><p> And now I know why -- despite all this laudable backstory--the show itself is rarely performed.</p><p> We begin with a desperate woman on the streets, ready to sell herself for half a buck.</p><p> But the man Mol solicits says that's too much.</p><p> What's a human's body worth? Not much more than a human's dignity, apparently.</p><p> A cop breaks up this sad affair, only to pressure Moll (LaraPulver, who played the woman from Sherlock ) into having sex with him.</p><p> She refuses, he tosses her into the clink and a passel of local dignitaries are bundled into jail as well.</p><p> They're anonymous members of the Liberty Committee, middle-class toadies who do the bidding of Mr.</p><p> Mister in "Steeltown, U.S.A."
