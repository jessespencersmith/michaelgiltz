ORIGINAL POST FOR FINAL NIGHT OF PERFORMANCE COMPETITION:Well, Kris Allen gets to join Clay Aiken and David Archuleta as Idol contestants who never were put into the bottom two but failed to win it all.Clearly, Adam Lambert will be the next American Idol.</p><p> I've been a big fan of Kris and he certainly has the potential to deliver a great album sometime down the road.</p><p> But Adam had a much better mix of songs that contrasted each other perfectly and let him show off his versatility.</p><p> It was fittingthat Carrie Underwood closed the show for not since her triumphal march to victory has one contestant so clearly dominated a season.</p><p> And just like Underwood, Adam Lambert will probably prove to be wildly commercial as well. (In this day and age, that would mean going triple platinum.) As for the judges, the men stayed true to form but the women were not.</p><p> Paula has been sharp and insightful all season but tonight she stayed firmly in cheerleader mode.</p><p> Kara did herself no favorsby being strident and wildly intense the entire night.</p><p> Every comment was delivered with ferocious determination.</p><p> Relax, Kara! SONG ONE -- Kris won the toss and elected to go second.</p><p> Adam chose to reprise the Tears For Fears song "Mad World," which was smart.</p><p> The production was over the top with so much blue lighting and fog that you couldn't even see Adam's face for the first 30 seconds.</p><p> He sang the tunevery well, not notably better or worse than his excellent original rendition.</p><p> All the judges praised him but it wasn't as emotionally piercing as the first time around and Simon was right to find the theatricality a bit much (especially for this quiet tune).</p><p> Kris sang "Ain't No Sunshine," again, thesong I would have chosen for him.</p><p> He did it perhaps a tad better.</p><p> The longer performance time let him stretch out the tune and nail that bridge yet again.</p><p> Plus, the camera was in tight on Kris the whole time.</p><p> They were both strong but on production and performance I'd give a slight edge toKris.</p><p> SONG TWO -- Adam was asked to perform "A Change Is Gonna Come" by Sam Cooke, a terrific selection.</p><p> It also had a great arrangement that included bluesy guitar to give it some bite.</p><p> Adam just nailed it to the wall, making the most out of a dramatic ballad that builds and builds.</p><p> He used his big voice for that ultra-dramatic wailing and in this case it worked perfectly.</p><p> Like the first songof his, it's slow at first.</p><p> But it really builds to a big finale so it's a good contrast.</p><p> Plus his outfitlooked classy and good.</p><p> Finally, both this and "Mad World" are very emotionally resonant for Adam, so that helps too.</p><p> He ends with a giant Idol moment and I immediately wrote down "He's won it all." But then, I thought the same thing after Clay Aiken sang "Bridge Over Troubled Water." Kris is asked to sing Marvin Gaye's "What's Going On," which does NOT seem like a good idea to me.</p><p> He shows off his cool, mellow demeanor and strums away on an acoustic guitar.</p><p> The performance is actually better than I feared.</p><p> But it's way too similar in tone and style to the firstnumber.</p><p> And now he has to sing the presumably dull new Idol theme? Not looking good.</p><p> SONG THREE -- "No Boundaries." As Adam begins to sing, I immediately realize how he can knock out a silly power ballad pop song like this in his sleep.</p><p> The sillier the lyrics, the more he canjust wail away.</p><p> And this tune has plenty of silly lyrics: "With every step you climb another mountain" and "Just when you think the road is going nowhere" and "You can go higher, you can go deeper" and so on.</p><p> Stuff and nonsense but Adam gives it passion and it is more upbeat and pop,so it again makes a very good contrast with the other tunes he sang.</p><p> Kris gives it a sort of Bryan Adams feel which is a good call.</p><p> After being flat at the start, he gets into a groove.</p><p> But this is still a much better fit with Adam.</p><p> Ultimately, Kris's three tunes blended together, each one ending on a fairly mellow note.</p><p> Adam on the other hand got to show a lot of his skills.</p><p> Plus he gave the single best performance of the nightwith "A Change Is Gonna Come." That's the one people will remember and that's the one they'llvote for.</p><p> But as Paula would say, neither of them are losers.</p><p> And certainly not the viewers, when we had the most talented duo in the finale since Ruben Studdard and Clay Aiken faced off.</p><p> Both could definitely deliver the goods creatively.</p><p> Kris will probably benefit by not winning since that's coolerin the indie rock, John Mayer world.</p><p> Plus, he would have gotten a lot of grief if Adam was seen tohave been cheated out of his rightful crown.</p><p> As for Adam, I hope he takes a cue from the very first Pop Idol winner, Will Young.</p><p> He came out right after winning and no one blinked an eye and he has gone on to have exceptional ongoing success in the UK as a singer and actor.
