Never -- and I say this with love -- never has a TV drama been so good and then gone so completelyoff the rails as Friday Night Lights.</p><p> I loved thefirst season of this drama about a small Texastown where the thwarted and mundane lives ofseemingly everyone was channeled into a passionfor the high school football team.</p><p> Beautifullyacted with a great cast, respectful of the faith thatinforms many people's lives, filled with subtletyand charm, sexy as hell -- Friday Night Lights had it all.</p><p> Including low ratings.</p><p> Perhaps out of panic, Season Two began with a show stopper of a plot twist -- one character defending themself and clearly frightened out of their mind killed a vicious rapist who had promised to attack again.</p><p> This turn of events soon devolved into idiotic dumping of the body, burning cars to hide evidence and other shenanigans.</p><p> But this much-debated twist was only the tip of the iceberg.</p><p> Virtually every character suddenly began behaving like a bonehead.</p><p> The engaging young couple (the stammering, sweet high school quarterback and his smart, coach's daughter girlfriend) split up for no reason.</p><p> The coach's wife -- always a rock of intelligence and humor and wit and warmth -- became a desperate, weeping wimp collapsing under the pressure of a new baby.</p><p> The coach's new job at a major university was clearly just a stunt and it was only a matter of episodes before he returned to town.</p><p> The paralyzed quarterback - a smart, driven man destined to succeed -- decided to go to Mexico and let a fly-by-night surgeon with seedy offices on the second floor of a run-down building operate on his spine.</p><p> And on and on and on.</p><p> Every week I yelled back at the screen as people I'd come to know behaved completely out of character.</p><p> And every week I came back.</p><p> Despite the major stumbles in the scripts, this is the bestensemble on TV right now.</p><p> And they still managed to wring some genuine emotion and truth out ofthe most soap opera-like random twists.</p><p> It's a testament to the emotional investment people makein a series that they'll keep coming back
