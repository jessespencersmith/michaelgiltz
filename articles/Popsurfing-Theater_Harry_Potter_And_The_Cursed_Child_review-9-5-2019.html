HARRY POTTER AND THE CURSED CHILD *** out of **** THE CIRCLE SERIES AT CIRCLE IN THE SQUARE It's been on Broadway for 17 months.</p><p> Friends of mine saw it even earlier in London.</p><p> The acclaimed original cast has moved on.</p><p> And nowI've FINALLY seen Harry Potter and the Cursed Child.</p><p> A few shows each year slip through my grasp and this was one of them -- a shame, since I know the books backwards and forwards and have impressed nieces and nephews for years with the fact that I interviewed J.K.Rowling many, many years ago.</p><p> But times are tight and money is tootight to mention so what could I do? I entered the digital lottery atTodayTix .</p><p> And then I entered it the next week.</p><p> And the next and the next and some 80 or so drawings later, I won!! Here are somethoughts.</p><p> THE LYRIC IS GORGEOUS -- Once a barn of a theater, the Lyric was the home of Spiderman-Man: Turn Off The Dark.</p><p> That felt more like a circus than a musical and the cavernous space felt appropriate.</p><p> But it's been completely renovated for this show.</p><p> The back wall hasbeen moved forward to make it somewhat more intimate. (It nowseats some 1,600 people, rather than 1,866 seats at its peak.) Andliterally every detail is thoughtful and handsome, from the "H" stitched into the carpeting to the two levels of box seats on the side a la the opera and just...everything.</p><p> It's so handsome that one weeps tothink they may have to un-renovate it for landmark reasons when theshow leaves.</p><p> On the bright side, that won't happen for years and years.</p><p> THE PLAY WORKS FOR MUGGLES -- Like I said, I know the books quite well and saw all the films and even took the test at Pottermore to determine I belong in House Ravenclaw if and when I get a scholarship to Hogwarts.</p><p> I also read the script for the play whenit was published and thought, Gee, this won't make a lick of sense to anyone who HASN'T read the books or seen the movies.</p><p> Not quite.</p><p> MyMICHAEL GILTZ AT WORK Michael Giltz is a freelance writer based in NYC and can be reached atmgiltz@pipeline.com FAVORITE LINKS Americablog Five O'Clock Lightning baseball blogDeep Pop -- Lori Lakin's Blog The Back Page -- Jason Page on ESPN Radio Cine-Blog -- George Robinson's Blog Documents On Art & Cinema - Daryl Chin's Blog Brucie G's Wondrous Blog Of Adventure and Mystery -- Bruce Greenspan's Blog BLOG ARCHIVE ▼ 2019 (58) ▼ September (9) SHOWBIZ SANDBOX #463: Why Is A TV Specialguest had read the first book and seen a few of the early movies.</p><p> So he wasn't a novice exactly but he certainly isn't steeped in Rowling.</p><p> The show worked a charm for him.</p><p> After each act, he did tend to have a brief question or two about a reference made by this or that character.I happily expounded at geeky length.</p><p> Undoubtedly, the play will be farmore powerful for those who do love the books.</p><p> But arrive fiveminutes early and read the summaries of the books (or just Book 8) and you'll be fine.</p><p> NO BUTTER BEER? -- The merchandise was copious and good- looking. But surely my seats would be in the top tier behind a pole or something? Well, my heart did sink a bit when I saw them.</p><p> I had tickets in the second level (the dress circle) in row G all the way to theside in seats 9 and 11.</p><p> Only three rows remained between me and theback wall.</p><p> And ALL the way to the side.</p><p> This is a play, not a musical.</p><p> And even if I was seeing the tap-dance extravaganza 42nd Street again, I'd much rather be in the orchestra than high up.</p><p> Then Iactually went to the theater and took my seats.</p><p> The sight lines wereimpeccable and while Harry Potter has a lot of intimate, quiet scenes, it's certainly a big show with a lot of spectacle.</p><p> I would happily buy those seats again and for more money.</p><p> Frankly, I don't think any seat in dress circle would be bad.</p><p> Only a few people in the boxes and wayalong the side in row A might have missed a tiny moment of action inCelebrat...</p><p> SHOWBIZ SANDBOX #462: Fall Film Festival Roundup MOVIES: The Scarily Good Combination Of Hitchcock ...</p><p> MOVIES: A "Close Encounter" With A Steven Spielber...</p><p> THEATER: "Betrayal" And The (Permanent?) HOW WAS THE BLOODY SHOW? -- Great fun.</p><p> Harry Potter and the Cursed Child read a little roughly for me. (I'm not very good about reading plays or film scripts and imagining what can be done with them.) I loved the set-up.</p><p> The sons of Harry Potter and Draco Malfoy head to Hogwarts and positively HATE it.</p><p> And when they become friends, their parents positively hateTHAT.</p><p> Then things get complicated.</p><p> On the page, it seemed the needto get the band back together (i.e. make Harry, Ron and Hermione the center of things) took precedence over a fresh new story.</p><p> On the stage, it more cohesive, with the stories of the parents and childrenintertwining from the start.</p><p> The smartest decision they made was toevoke a classic, Victorian-style of stage craft.</p><p> No video or projections here.</p><p> This show could have been staged as-is a hundred years ago.</p><p> And the movement/choreography of Steven Hoggett deservedly made thisthe rare drama to be nominated for Best Choreography. (It shouldhave won, actually.) He and director John Tiffany create real magic ina thousand little ways, alongside the rest of the crack technical team.It's a genuine treat to watch.</p><p> Heck, even before the show begins, the stage just LOOKS gorgeous.</p><p> And while most of the effects are dead simple (and doubly effective for being so), I haven't a clue how theymake the entire stage seem to waver and shimmy and shake whentime travel comes into play.</p><p> The original cast was acclaimed to high heavens and while I can't single out anyone in the new cast, I don't see any huge weaknesses,either.</p><p> They are capable and strong, even if the action by the secondhalf of part two gets tense and the show's idea of drama is for theactors to get a bit shouty. "My son is missing!" shouts one character."SO IS MINE!" shouts another.</p><p> Somehow, the melodrama adds to the fun.</p><p> And it IS fun.</p><p> I haven't enjoyed an audience's reaction to a show this much since seeing The Lion King.</p><p> That musical flips the audience out right at the start with the brilliant "Circle Of Life" number, a show-stopper to end all show-stoppers.</p><p> Little kids stood on their seats andlooked all around them positively wide-eyed with wonder.</p><p> Heck, I didthe same thing.</p><p> And at Harry Potter and the Cursed Child? People (not just kids) positively gasped at various revelations.</p><p> They shivered in fear.</p><p> They smiled with fondness over a shared memory voiced by someone on stage.</p><p> When one character ventured down the center aisleof the theater, virtually everyone in the dress circle craned their neckor just stood up to peer down and watch -- they were desperate not tomiss a moment.</p><p> When one character walked quietly on stage for the first time, the reaction was so visceral and shocked my guest was beside himself with desire to ask me who it was.</p><p> And that's not all.</p><p> Atthe end of the first intermission and again at the end of Part One(surely the only time a stage play has ever stopped with the words "tobe continued" flashed onto the curtain) and again at the intermission of Part Two, the creators stage both a visual spectacle and introduce ahead-spinning plot twist that left the audience positively gobsmacked and bursting with chatter.</p><p> It's a beautifully crafted production of a not-bad play, catnip for Potter fans and fun enough to send newbies to the books to see what all thefuss is about.</p><p> The stagecraft is impeccable and a joy to watch.</p><p> WhileJK Rowling only worked on the story (the script is by Jack Thorne), it shows yet again how wisely she has made use of her fame.</p><p> Rather than thinning out the pleasure of those seven books by writing more HarryPotter adventurers, Rowling uses her imagination and commercialpower to explore new worlds and new forms of storytelling.</p><p> The website Pottermore is quite innovative.</p><p> The theme park rides are top- notch.</p><p> The prequel movies dubbed Fantastic Beasts are at least commercially successful and Rowling is improving as a screenwriter.</p><p> And now this stage show is a triumph that will surely be many a child'sintroduction to the pleasures of theater.</p><p> All in all, that's prettymagical.</p><p> THE CIRCLE SERIES AT CIRCLE IN THE SQUARE You don't need to win the lottery to enjoy the pleasure of live theater, even in New York City.</p><p> All over the city, you'll find staged readings,semi-staged productions of little-known musicals and cabaret shows presenting songs for a musical someone would like to make someday.</p><p> And it's all far more accessible and inexpensive than a ticket toBroadway.</p><p> Adding to the fun is Circle In The Square's recently launched Circle Series.</p><p> Throughout the summer and fall, they offer readings of newand classic works, asking only a suggested donation of $20 for adultsand $10 for seniors and students (meaning you won't be shamed if youcan only chip in say $5 or just a smile).</p><p> All proceeds benefit the Circle In The Square Theatre School, which makes sense since students and alumni often people the casts.</p><p> It's presented in partnership with thegreedily named Pigasus Institute.</p><p> I recently attended a reading of The Philadelphia Story by Philip Barry and it was quite fun.</p><p> Cocktails start at 6 pm and the readingsstarts at 7 pm.</p><p> The film starring Katherine Hepburn, Cary Grant andJimmy Stewart is an all-time favorite of mine, but the play is slightlydifferent and I'd never read it or seen it done.</p><p> I think the film madesmart choices and improved on the play, so if anyone is going to stage it again, they should start with the film script.</p><p> And indeed they should stage it again because the story works like a charm.</p><p> The Circle Series does exactly what you want it to do.</p><p> It lets you enjoy some performances for a very modest price, spot some new talent you'll want to keep an eye on and maybe introduce you to a new playwright of worth.</p><p> Three more shows are on tap this season,
