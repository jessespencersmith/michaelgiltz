Re-doing his action flick Ashes Of Time feels like a momentary exercise.</p><p> An English language remake of the not so great Lady From Shanghai seems a spotty future plan.</p><p> And then there's My Blueberry Nights ($19.98; Weinstein).</p><p> This poorly received film stars Norah Jones as a nice girl who indulges in pie at her local dinerwhen her heart is broken.</p><p> Bizarrely, she has to go on an elaborate road trip in order to understandthat said diner owner -- the wildly adorable Jude Law -- might just be boyfriend material.</p><p> It has allthe usual Wong tics: slow motion, overwhelmingly gorgeous cinematography, a beautiful cast,music that dominates the proceedings and precious, hyper-aware narration.</p><p> And there's theproblem.</p><p> It's not just that My Blueberry Nights fails (filming in a second language is always a challenge), it's that the film fails so spectacularly while embracing the very style that made Wongfamous.</p><p> Not only do you reject this film, but it forces you to rethink your opinion of his earlier work.</p><p> Often, a movie that drips sentimentality or preciousness can get away with it...in Italian or Norwegian or some other tongue.</p><p> You can bet critics that praise certain foreign language filmswould never stand for their open emotionality if the movie were American.</p><p> It's not self-hatred --it's just that certain emotions or certain artifices are easier to swallow with the slight distance that Be the First to Submit This Story to Digg Like this story?
