She looked very cute and immediately struck me as looking confident.</p><p> A rough last note was a shame because I thought she was pretty good overall.</p><p> In the race to be Adele or Duffy (or Madeleine Peyroux) I thought she outpaced Lilly Scott tonight.</p><p> SIOBHAN MAGNUS -- Sang "Wicked Game" by Chris Isaak." I was so happy before the break when Ryan Seacrest announced she'd be singing Isaak.</p><p> He has a catalog of terrific pop tunes that are immediately catchy a la Roy Orbison, which are perfect for mining on the show and yet not so familiarthat they would seem obvious.</p><p> Unfortunately, Siobhan chose the obvious tune, his only Top 40 hit and one that can be pretty draggy in unsafe hands.</p><p> Siobhan had stood out for me so I'm bummed she went the dowdy, cocktail lounge route in her rendition, though she built a little steam on the emphatic bridge.But overall, too slow.</p><p> Plus, the band sounded like it was in the next room (as it did on most tunes, obviously a choice by the producers).</p><p> On the bright side, she had some cute friends in the audience.</p><p> CRYSTAL BOWERSOX -- Sang Alanis Morissette's #15 hit "Hand In My Pocket." Puts her way, way, way out front of the others in my book.</p><p> The judges were morereserved when I would have given her a standing O right off the bat.</p><p> Not a brilliant vocal or stunningly original arrangement but it felt fresh and genuine and real and I wished she could have kept singing.</p><p> KATIE STEVENS -- Sang "Feelin' Good" by (as Ryan Seacrest put it) MIchael Buble.</p><p> No one's ever hit the Hot 100 with it, but surely this number from the Anthony Newley/Leslie Bricusse musical The Roar Of The Greasepaint -- The Smell Of The Crowd still belongs to Nina Simone if anyone.</p><p> The 17-year-old looked too dolled up, which made the whole number feel like play-acting to me, like a girl dressing up and singing along in her bedroom.</p><p> I immediately wrote down that Simon would tell her to act her age...but Ellen beat him to it.</p><p> Randy correctly pointed out that her big problem was oversinging notes and going sharp (is it my imagination or is he stepping it up this season?) and Simon nailed it by calling herperformance "pageant-y."
