Yep.</p><p> In it, the commanding Penelope Cruz (really still coming into her own as an actress and star) plays the secretary to a powerful man.</p><p> With an ailing father, she is compromised into becoming his mistress.Meanwhile, many years later we get the story of the film director who fell in love with her during the making of her first (and only) movie.</p><p> The director is now blind, his single assistant has a son with an absent mysterious father (hmmm), and the powerful man's gay son now calls himself RayX and wants to make a film with the blind director turned writer.</p><p> A light melodrama, none of thesoapy elements here will sound surprising or unexpected.</p><p> What is unexpected is how little is done with it.</p><p> Even fans of Almodovar will be nonplussed by the story.</p><p> Why do artists so often assume their mature years should be devoted to serious work? Two stories within the story here indicatethat -- if it interested him enough -- Almodovar could make a great comedy or genre piece that would be tremendous fun.</p><p> The son of the assistant describes the plot for a vampire movie that sounds hilarious.</p><p> And then we see what seems like roughly five minutes of the movie within themovie called Girls With Suitcases, which is an homage to Women On The Verge Of A Nervous Breakdown.</p><p> Both of these glimpses of other projects are far lighter and funnier and more compelling than anything else in the film.</p><p> Show me that movie is the invariable response of the Sarah Jessica Parker and Matthew Broderick RevealTwins' Names READ MORE Robert Pattinson Films by Day, Prowls by Night READ MORE Gosselins Go Their Separate Ways READ MORE More Celebrity News at People.com Don't Miss HuffPost Bloggers Gavin Newsom The Nation's Most Ambitious Recycling Law T.</p><p> Boone Pickens Propane and the Pickens Plan Popular Stories on HuffPost Johnny Depp As MadHatter, HelenaBIG NEWS : Jon & Kate Plus 8 | Celebrity Skin | Tom Cruise | Celebrity Kids | More...</p><p> LOG IN | SIGN UP Be the First to Submit This Story to Digg Get Breaking News Alerts Share Print Comments never spam Michael Giltz: Cannes 2009 Day Seven: More Almodovar Is Less http://www.huffingtonpost.com/michael-giltz/cannes-2009-day-seven-mor_b_205552.html [6/23/2009 5:57:45 PM] audience.</p><p> You can grow older without growing up; it's a pity Almodovar hasn't learned that yet.</p><p> Les Herbes Folles * 1/2 -- Director Alain Resnais has delivered one of the most perplexing films of the fest.</p><p> Lars Von Trier's Anti-Christ has nothing on this one when it comes to throwing the audience for a loop.</p><p> One hour into this film, I defy anyone to describe it accurately and know where it's headed.</p><p> Is it a light, witty romantic comedy; a searching drama; a nihilistic fable and/or asatire of any or all of these? It begins with playful narration describing a woman who is buying shoes only to have her purse stolen.</p><p> That purse is discovered by a mature gentleman who hesitates, picks it up and takes it to the police.</p><p> Very small moments can lead to momentous changes, we aretold (again and again and again).</p><p> The man becomes mildly obsessed with the woman.</p><p> Then thewoman becomes obsessed with the man.</p><p> Then they both turn on each other (though never at thesame time).</p><p> The wife of the man is of course at least 30 years younger and stunningly beautiful and yet still doesn't blink an eye when his mistress comes over for a chat.</p><p> This is a French film after all.</p><p> And constantly the mood is changing.</p><p> A playful moment is followed by a harshly real moment of emotion and pain which is followed by something entirely else.</p><p> And it all ends on a completely discontinuous, seemingly unrelated and very brief moment that left everyone shaking their headsin confusion.</p><p> Some of the French partisans in the audience applauded but no one booed becausethey were still so bewildered.</p><p> Here is my explanation: Spoiler SpoilerIn the last scene, a small plane with our main characters is plunging out of control towards the earth.</p><p> Suddenly, the camera zooms over the landscape and into a home where a little girl is askingher mother if -- when she comes back in another life as a cat -- she can have some cat treats.</p><p> And the film ends.</p><p> I should add, several other scenes in the last few minutes included "Fin!" prominently displayed on the screen so no one can be blamed for not being certain when the movieactually did come to a halt.</p><p> The only explanation for this moment is to show the little girl's life cut short.</p><p> Her time has ended just as the lives of the people in the plane ended.</p><p> And why? Because the zipper on some pants broke. (That led to complications which led to the seeming crash.) See? Thetiniest incident can change your world.</p><p> That may not be an accurate reading but it's the only onewe can think of and is the only way to put this fitfully amusing but confused trifle to rest.</p><p> End of Spoiler End of SpoilerI Love You, Phillip Morris ** -- Is it possible to over-emphasize chemistry?
