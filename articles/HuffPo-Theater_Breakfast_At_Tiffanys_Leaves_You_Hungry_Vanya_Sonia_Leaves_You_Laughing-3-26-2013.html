Othersprefer the darker, more complex novella by Truman Capote.</p><p> Neither group, sadly, will enjoy theBroadway staging of a play that hopes to combine the glamor of the movie and the richness of the novellabut falls short of both.</p><p> I can't tell you how many people have asked me or assumed this was a musical, but it's a straight drama.</p><p> Like the novella, it focuses on Fred (Cory Michael Smith), a struggling writer who is pulled into the orbitof Holly Golightly (Emilia Clarke), a backwoods teenager who has reinvented herself as an intriguing,glamorous figure of high society.</p><p> Holly is dazzling and fun and irresponsible and determined to keepreinventing herself until she finds someone or somewhere that she likes.</p><p> Fred is the downstairs neighborwho knows exactly what he wants -- to write and be read -- but he enjoys the transformative friendship Holly offers.</p><p> It's the eternal story of New York City: people following their dreams and lost souls who follow any dream they can, each one more glittering and improbable than the next.</p><p> MOST POPULAR '8' Expectations: Arguments CastDoubt On GayMarriage Ban 'Real Housewives OfAtlanta' StarHeaded ForDivorce?
