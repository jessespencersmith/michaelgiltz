novelty.Actually, I'm a newbie, somehow never having been to a Fuerza Bruta event before.</p><p> So even though this editionreportedly recycles quite a few stunts from previous FB shows of the past, it was all new to me.</p><p> Nonetheless,the almost determined lack of imagination in each bit (calling them skits would be pushing it), the avoidance ofalmost anything resembling choreography or humor, the refusal to do anything other than the minimal makes theevening a joyless affair for all but the most un-jaded 17 year old.It begins and ends with anonymous tribalistic, techno music followed by a group of performers who float over thecrowd in a big clump, waving and yelling enthusiastically while swinging back and forth.</p><p> Though there is nothingchallenging in the stunts performed (this is not a display of gymnastic or acrobatic skills of any note), it doesdemand the cast be athletic and energetic and they certainly did their best to rouse the crowd into a funatmosphere.</p><p> Still, this bit set the tone.</p><p> Like the worst Saturday Night Live skits of the past 20 years, it offerssome sliver of an idea (in this case, the cast dressed in "regular" clothes swinging out over the crowd) butinstead of developing the idea and coming up with twists or a way to up the ante, they just repeat it a few timesand then stop.Other bits occur.</p><p> Two women doing wire work (arguably the most challenging of the evening) run across acurtained wall encircling the audience, screaming and yelling as if something momentous were about to occur.They do a few somersaults, each one running to one end and then the other.</p><p> Like most everything else in theshow and what you see in the trailer above, it looks vaguely cool for a moment until you realize...that's it.The Man On The Treadmill is apparently a perennial feature and it comes the closest to what Wayra might havebeen if it actually tried to be something.</p><p> Nothing actually happens but it does feel as if the skit progresses towardsomething.</p><p> A man with a really bad commute is walking determinedly on a treadmill.</p><p> It speeds up and he keepsgoing with Buster Keaton-like determination.</p><p> Attendants place chairs and tables on the treadmill which hedodges while moving forward, always forward.</p><p> People jump on the platform and he bumps and dodges pastthem.</p><p> Eventually, a "brick wall" of cardboard boxes appears but he bursts through that too.</p><p> He's shot in thechest, but even this doesn't stop him.</p><p> On he goes.</p><p> As with so much of the show, once I got the idea, I foundmore pleasure in observing how the technical team kept things running smoothly than in the actual skit.</p><p> Still, thiswas definitely as close to art as they got, though it served mostly to put the rest of the show in stark contrast.A pool interlude was a happy oasis in an evening that at least pretended to create an aura of tension andmayhem.</p><p> In this bit, a plastic sort of swimming pool descends from the ceiling.</p><p> We look up through the bottomand see a woman lit up.</p><p> Again, it looks sort of cool.</p><p> A
