BLACK SWAN -- ** 1/2 out of **** More people have probably seen movies about the world of ballet than have ever actually gone to a ballet.</p><p> And the storyline is often the same: young rising star displaces the (relatively) aging prima ballerina ofthe past.</p><p> Throw in a dalliance with a magnetic choreographer along with bruised feet and bodies and backstage drama and you've got your film.</p><p> Black Swan follows this pattern to a t, with one vital difference.</p><p> It's shot like a suspense or horror film and our heroine is having a mental breakdown of sortswhile she desperately tries to prepare for the lead role in Swan Lake.</p><p> Natalie Portman is the dancer, a virginal young woman who is perfect as the tentative, scared but lovely White Swan.</p><p> But her choreographer/director also wants her to embody the sensual, even dangerous Black Swan and this issimply beyond her.</p><p> One peek at Portman's bedroom, which is filled with stuffed animals and looks like the room of a 13 year old, and you know she's got a long way to go.</p><p> Mila Kunis is the rival who can embody danger with ease (she has a lot of fun) and Winona Ryder is the displaced star who bitterlytaunts her replacement.</p><p> Ir's all so conventional in plot, but director Darren Aronofsky keeps us offbalance by wondering exactly how nutty Portman is getting.</p><p> Did she really make love to Kunis or was that a a dream? How edgy can a child who still lives with her mother and has never had sex actually be? When her director (a ripe Vincent Cassel) gives her a homework assignment of touching herself, of livinga little, we just know she's gonna get an incomplete.</p><p> Great for dance lovers who have watched The RedShoes a thousand times, Black Swan is enjoyably over-the-top in the midst of its deadly serious exploration of artists who push themselves to succeed.</p><p> It's so nutty you can't help respecting it.</p><p> But if you're not longing to join (or at least attend) the ABT, this may seem overheated melodrama.</p><p> And it is, itis.</p><p> Melodrama made with care and acted to the hilt.TOP VIDEO PICKS Kourtney K.'s Stunning Bikini Bod Jane Krakowski RevealsBaby Bump Bristol Palin's New RadioGig? 1 of 8 MOST DISCUSSED RIGHT NOW HOT ON FACEBOOK HOT ON TWITTER Natalie Portman Television Brazil Sudan Christianity CareersHUFFPOST'S BIG NEWS PAGES More Celebrity News at Popeater.com One And Done: 'Sarah Palin's Alaska' NOT Renewed: Report 42 Comments Natalie Portman: I Was 'Barely Eating' During 'Black Swan' 435 Comments Camille Grammer's Porn Past Comes Back To Haunt Her 969 Comments PHOTOS: Olivia Wilde Dons See-Through Bikini 101 Comments Amanda Seyfried, Ryan Phillippe Dating: Romance Heats Up 4 Comments Ashton Kutcher: Training, Paranoid About Real Life 'End Of Days' 904 CommentsJACK GOES BOATING ** out of **** Actor Philip Seymour Hoffman has been a big supporter of playwright Bob Glaudini so it's logical for Hoffman to make his feature directorial debut with this small ensemble work in which he co-starredalongside John Ortiz and Daphne Rubin-Vega. THE ILLUSIONIST *** 1/2 out of **** I just loved the animated film The Triplets of Belleville by director Sylvain Chomet. (Didn't everyone?) His follow-up is both a delight and a reminder of what a narrow terrain most animated films cover.</p><p> It's based on an unproduced screenplay by Jacques Tati, the last of the (essentially) silent movie clowns.</p><p> Icall him a silent clown in the tradition of Chaplin and Keaton, but Tati was also brilliant about the use ofsound in his films, even though dialogue was at a minimum.</p><p> I wondered early on in this film what soundscapes he might have created for this film.</p><p> It's set in the early 60s, when a middle-aged magician is touring the fading vaudeville circuit and rock and roll and old age are nipping at his heels.</p><p> Somehow he isadopted by a young girl (a teenager really on the cusp of adulthood) who almost believes he really can work magic.</p><p> They head off to the big city, where his job prospects get bleaker and bleaker (at one point he is performing in a department store window) and she experiences first love.</p><p> It's a gentle, simple tale andthe charm comes in the small details, like the smoke from the train that undulates up and out in the mostpleasing manner.</p><p> Or the Scottish man who dragoons the magician into performing in his small pub on a small island and is either constantly drunk or just drunk on life or both.</p><p> Ideally, you just get lost in a movie but of course it's inevitable that at times I can't help thinking about where it's headed and what Imight say about it.</p><p> At the halfway point, I was pleased but not wowed by The Illusionist, which seemed a worthy if slighter follow-up.</p><p> And then as it approached its bittersweet finale (that's no spoiler: the entirefilm is bittersweet), suddenly I found myself deeply moved and touched and even lifted up.</p><p> I'm not quitesure why or how it happened but it did.</p><p> That doesn't happen often at any movie, much less animated ones where pop culture references are usually the stock in trade.</p><p> Remember that five minute scene early on in Up, where we see an entire marriage take place in a montage that moved darn near everyone totears? Well, The Illusionist is quieter and less devastating, but it's emotions are no less real.</p><p> One of the best of the year.</p><p> NEVER LET ME GO ** out of **** Did I miss the Carey Mulligan backlash?
