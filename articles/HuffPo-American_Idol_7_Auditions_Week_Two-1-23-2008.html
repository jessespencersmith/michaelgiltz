was a renewed emphasis on the backstory of the kids auditioning, whichmeant lots of single parents, ill relatives, cute kids and most heart-tugging of all, cute kids withserious illnesses.</p><p> This "Queen For A Day" approach has always been there.</p><p> But forgive me forhoping the biggest sob stories don't get out of Hollywood -- I hate to think of people getting votesfor their personal tragedies and triumphs rather than for their singing.</p><p> Tuesday's notables: Tetiana Ostapouych -- She walked in looking smug and self-satisfied.</p><p> Ooh, I thought, our first villain if she proud of (that's not a slam; it's the one thing that keeps her looks from dullbeauty), she has the aggressive air of someone that can't let you appreciate them for whateverappeal they have because she already appreciates it before you.</p><p> Simon, as usual, nailed it when hesaid "Obnoxious!" after she left.</p><p> But fame-hungry artists often ARE obnoxious.</p><p> It's part of the drivethat keeps them going.</p><p> So keep your eye on Tetiana as one you might love to hate but which can'tbe denied if her singing is more versatile than it appears.</p><p> Perrie Cataldo -- a single dad who Cheap shot.</p><p> Monique Gibson -- here, they got the balance of how to treat a bad singer correctly.</p><p> Monique was dreadful, but the she seemed to take the judges' reactions in a good-natured way.</p><p> They were friendly but blunt and it all seemed a relatively OK wake-up call until suddenly tears startedstreaming down her face.</p><p> Randy and Paula and Simon all immediately comforted her and showedempathy and seemed taken aback since Monique had seemed stable.</p><p> The show caught the pathosof her not realizing how little talent she had without making fun of her.</p><p> Samantha Musa -- this was the singer whose sister peched on Simon's lap.</p><p> She sang Aretha and had a rough high note but was pretty good.Blake Boshnack -- here Idol wrongly celebrates obsessive determination.</p><p> This guy has auditioned in about ten cities, including the time he dressed up as the Statue of Liberty (a moment Simon rightly ended after about two notes).</p><p> He sang "Stand By Me" and while Blake probably killed at his own bar mitzvah it was just absurd and sad for him (and his mom) to think he has a talent for anything more than karaoke.</p><p> This was followed immediately by one of the new Idol songs -- sung by Rod Stewart over footage of Blake -- presumably meant for the montage scene when asinger is sent home.</p><p> It included lines about how you should "never give up on your dream." Actually, that sentiment -- and the celebration of Blake's misguided, repeated auditions -- should be the OPPOSITE of the what Idol is about.</p><p> In fact, you SHOULDN'T pursue your dream if your dream is playing in the NBA and you're 5 feet 7 inches tall and can't sink a basketball.</p><p> The constant message of the show (in ways both mean and kind) is that it's not ENOUGH to want to be a "star."You have to have talent.</p><p> But that's not enough either.</p><p> You also have to work really, really hard.</p><p> Andthat's not usually enough either.</p><p> You also need a little luck.</p><p> If the judges say you suck and willnever, ever be a professinal singer, that doesn't mean you should "persevere." It means you shouldfind a new dream and hopefully rethink the allure of fame.</p><p> Talk about mixed messages.</p><p> David Archuleta -- a sweet-looking 16 year old kid who has the heartwarming backstory of fighting vocal paralysis.</p><p> That's nice.</p><p> His audition -- John Mayer's "Waiting On The World To Change" -- was pretty good, even though he forgot some of the words. (Oddly, I don't think thejudges mentioned this.</p><p> Even more oddly, Simon praised this as a great choice of song even thoughit's been done on Idol before so hardly counts as groundbreaking.) He was also nervous, tentative, and failed to make eye contact very much.</p><p> Decent voice but needs a few years ofseasoning and maturity.</p><p> But he's somewhat Tiger beat-ish and the judges rushed him through,which seemed to have as much to do with his backstory as his performance.</p><p> Carly Smithson -- an Irish lass who auditioned in a previous season and made it to Hollywood but her visa expired before she could go.</p><p> That's a pretty devastating backstory, though what the Idol producers failed to disclose was that Carly later signed a major label record deal and had millions spent on her debut CD before it all fell apart.</p><p> That doesn't have quite the same tragic airabout it, does it? I don't mind backup singers and people who've been on the brink auditioning(even though it doesn't feel quite in the spirit of the show).</p><p> But I MUCH prefer full disclosure.</p><p> Ifyour band had a record deal and released a single on a major label, if you won Star Search when you were younger, if you had a hit single in Japan, I want to know.</p><p> In fact, I need to know and Ifeel cheated if I discover this later.</p><p> Full disclosure, Idol producers.</p><p> Carly is going to Hollywood.</p><p> Good, she has a fine voice.</p><p> But she certainly knows the way there, in every sense and we shouldknow that in advance.</p><p> Style On CBS' "Washington Unplugged" Arianna was on CBS' "Washington...</p><p> Huff TV Arianna Appears on ABC's "This Week" Roundtable With George Stephanopoulos(VIDEO) Arianna was a guest this morning on...
