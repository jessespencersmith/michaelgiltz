ABC) is about a young lawyer who either has a brain tumor or is a prophet and is seeng visions that lead him to court cases that need hishelp.</p><p> Oh and each episode the cast breaks into song, usually a George Michael song, sometimeswith George Michael himself present.</p><p> Jonny Lee Miller is delightfully compelling as Stone and theabsurd premise somehow...worked.</p><p> The cases each week proved satisfying and the cast is so strong-- from Loretta Devine to Victor Garber -- that the wackiest twists are grounded by real people.</p><p> I'ma little worried by the ending of season one, which hints at an even more messianic role for Stone.But they've won my trust and I can't wait to see what happens next.</p><p> Pushing Daisies First Season($29.98; Warner Bros -- out September 16) seemed even more of a longshot.</p><p> This "detective" showfeatures a guy with a pie store and a magical gift -- if he touches a dead person, they come back tolife briefly.</p><p> Touch 'em again and they're dead for good -- if he doesn't touch them again, someoneelse dies.</p><p> Ned (Lee Pace) and Emerson Cod (Chi McBride) use that gift to solve crimes.</p><p> Along forthe ride is Chuck (Anna Friel), Ned's childhood sweetheart who he brought back to life and then letlive.</p><p> If he ever touches her again, she'll die, so their romance is adorably platonic.</p><p> Where to begin with this show? The cleverest set designs on TV, with one new location after another as quirky and oddball as any Tim Burton movie? The engaging narration by Jim Dale (who did the audio book versions of Harry Potter) and strikes the perfect tone of whimsy? The great cast, with Friel ascrewball heroine, Pace adorable, McBride a revelation and Kristin Chenoweth as good as always as a waitress pining for Ned? (The DVD also includes seven shorts.)
