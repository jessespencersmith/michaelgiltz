(I've reduced the characters to their racial identity because the film is so simple-minded.</p><p> That's how it plays; no one comes alive as an actual living and breathing character.) Virtually all the actors are weak.</p><p> An NGO worker spends all her time making a bemused/frustrated/admiring expression on her face when it comes to the dad.</p><p> In perhaps the most bizarre plot twist, the dad reaches the rebels, gets the boy, and isallowed to leave with two armed guards accompanying them.</p><p> Cut to them walking through thejungle and the dad jumps the guards and tries to knock them out, figuring he won't be let out alive.</p><p> But then they pull back the camera and instead of waiting till he was a mile or two away from the armed camp filled with trigger happy rebels, the dad has made his big move literally feet away from the cruel and vicious leader and all his minions.</p><p> Because why wait till you're out of sight of the enemy before jumping your armed guards? Laughable doesn't begin to cover it.</p><p> ENTER THE VOID * 1/2 -- Gasper Noe's follow-up to his terrific Irreversible is a nearly three hour glimpse at a young man who is shot by the police, arguably has his life flash before his eyes and then gets reincarnated as his sister's baby.</p><p> It's technically proficient, but once you've seen afly-over shot of Tokyo or rather once you've seen it eight or ten times the point of seeing it yet again begins to pale.</p><p> Two orphaned siblings are at the heart of the story: a sister who strips at a Tokyo bar and a brother who is dealing drugs.</p><p> At the very beginning of the film, overly aggressivepolice trying to bust him shoot the brother in the chest.</p><p> We soon get loads of flashbacks showing how he became a dealer, seeing their parents die in a car crash, watching the brother sleep with a friend's mom to get money, getting drugs from a dealer and on and on, all while our hero refersendlessly to The Tibetan Book Of The Dead in order to prepare us for the reincarnation.</p><p> The show- stopper here is certainly the shot of a penis entering a vagina...from the perspective of inside the vagina.</p><p> That brought roars of laughter from the assembled press, which at the end broke into divisive camps of loud hooting and strong applause.</p><p> To me, the movie is so simple-minded that no camerawork can save it.</p><p> Certainly the acting is affectless and flat, except for the brother and sisterplayed as little children -- those young actors are very nuanced and good.</p><p> It literally begins with death and ends with birth and I wanted to break out into "The Circle Of Life." A blank screen is thrown in towards the end for no apparent purpose other than to trick people into thinking theworld is over.</p><p> It began with a giant ENTER filling the sreen and ended with a giant THE and then VOID.</p><p> Surprising, it ain't.</p><p> It's conceivable that the film could be more effective at half the length and it would certainly be easy to do since there is endless repetition here that doesn't give it anycumulative power.</p><p> On the other hand, I stayed awake throughout the entire three hours and never felt antsy except on an intellectual level.</p><p> It's certainly well-crafted; it's just not well-thought out.</p><p> MAPS OF THE SOUNDS OF TOKYO No stars -- Enter The Void immediately looked better when I had to tackle this inert drama.</p><p> The entire story is set up in the first 20 minutes and after that virtually nothing happens.</p><p> A Spanish man in Tokyo has been dating the daughter of a businessexecutive; she commits suicide and writes in blood on her bathroom mirror the plea "Why couldn'tyou love me the way I love you?" Her father has a breakdown and tells an associate he can't bear the thought of a world in which his daughter is dead and the Spanish man is alive.</p><p> The businessman also blames his young associate, insisting that man should also have told the daughterhe loved her.</p><p> Meanwhile, the Spanish man is having great sex with a female fish monger/highly trained assassin (you read that right) but insists on telling her repeatedly that when he sleeps with her he is thinking of the dead girl.</p><p> Which kind of spoils the mood.</p><p> Extremely reserved, the fishmonger/trained assassin is friends with an old man who specializes in making sound recordings ofdaily life for the use of TV, radio and other media.</p><p> That's it.</p><p> The Spanish man ultimately leaves his wine shop in Tokyo to open a saki store in Spain.</p><p> The old man gives us ponderous narration undercut somewhat by the sight of a Night Ranger LP in his apartment looming over the old man'shead.</p><p> Not good.</p><p> TO DIE LIKE A MAN * -- The latest in a long line of tragic transvestites movies, this somber Children' READ MORE More Celebrity News at People.com Don't Miss HuffPost Bloggers Gavin Newsom The Nation's Most Ambitious Recycling Law T.</p><p> Boone Pickens Propane and the Pickens Plan Popular Stories on HuffPost Johnny Depp As Mad Hatter, HelenaBonham Carter's Red Queen And Anne Hathaway's WhiteQueen: FIRST LOOK (PHOTOS) The Tim Burton...</p><p> Mark Sanford: WhereIs He?
