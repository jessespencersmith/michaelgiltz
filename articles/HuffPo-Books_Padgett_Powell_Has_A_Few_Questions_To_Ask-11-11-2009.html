(Also some of his funniest, which is one reason Powell may have fallen out of favor for a while.</p><p> Funny is not appreciated in literary circles -- unless your name involves "Mark" and "Twain".) "I have never written a short story," insists Powell, who is divorced with two fine daughters, one an actress in Chicago and the other a graduate of a superior college and handy with firearms. "I've written some three page novels and some thirteen page novels.</p><p> I've written some novels predicated on such poorideas that they were over in three pages.</p><p> In fact, I was trying to sell a book before this one fluked out.And I call it Cries For Help: 45 Failed Novels.</p><p> Which is probably a better title than the book is.</p><p> To judge by its reception, it must be.
