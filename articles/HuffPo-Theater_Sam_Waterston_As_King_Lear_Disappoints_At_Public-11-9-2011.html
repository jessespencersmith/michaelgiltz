Entertainment News 3 12 0 2React Inspiring Funny Hot Scary Outrageous Amazing Weird CrazyTheater: Sam Waterston's "King Lear" Disappoints At Public KING LEAR ** out of **** PUBLIC THEATER King Lear is such a mountain of a play that just getting to the top must feel like an accomplishment.</p><p> But Sam Waterston is too good an actor to be satisfied with that.He surely wanted to reach the summit and find a freshperspective on the tale spread out before him.</p><p> After all, Waterston is an acclaimed stage actor and the star of one of the best TV dramas of all time ( I'll Fly Away, still bizarrely not on DVD).</p><p> Sadly, he has climbed Lear but he has not conquered it.</p><p> Since so much else of it is confused, I will lay the blame on the shoulders of director James MacDonald for this unfocused, unsuccessful effort.</p><p> Bursting with talent, this Lear feels as if it features a series of different actors giving different performances in different productions.</p><p> Minor details arrive and then disappear or simply make no sense in this story of a King who wants to divide his kingdom among his three daughters, angrily shoves aside his favorite when she refuses tostroke his ego and then goes mad when the other two snipe and tear at him with relentless hate.</p><p> Waterston exhibits signs of palsy or dementia early on, in the scene where his querulous, unreasonable Lear demands his daughters pay him homage.</p><p> It pops up a few more times, but doesn't seem part of anydetailed examination of what makes Lear behave so foolishly.</p><p> When the excellent actor see a Southern plantationLear , which presumably has been done before).</p><p> And yet, what the heck is a Southern black man doing with the rest of the characters, who all seem to be from an earlier British era in the fuzzy, Shakespeareanpast? Another minor character pops up in overalls with the accent of a farmer from the heartland.</p><p> When battle rages, we hear the sounds of World War I trench warfare for no particular reason.</p><p> This randomness infects the entire show.</p><p> I quite like the chain mail curtain that clinks and clanks every time someone enters and exits.</p><p> This curtain composed of chains hanging down moves forward and backward to open up or narrow the stage and would be ideal for a play dominated by war and is good here.</p><p> When it collapses into a pile that Learcan rest on, you are given the show's one modest flourish of originality.</p><p> Otherwise, the lighting and sound and costumes all feel adequate but uninspired since they've been given no direction.</p><p> The fight choreography by Thomas Schall is notably weak, though perhaps he was working with neophytes when itcomes to swordplay.</p><p> One struggles to say something positive.</p><p> No one is bad, just...indifferent.</p><p> Enid Graham and Kelli O'Hara have some fun with the viperish sisters.</p><p> But Kristen Connolly makes no impression as Cordelia and thegreat Bill Irwin seems utterly lost as the Fool, capering about in an ugly yellow costume and desperately mugging and vamping and trying to make sense of it all.</p><p> Michael McKean maintains his dignity as the duped Earl of Gloucester.</p><p> Michael Crane is fine in the role of Oswald until he's given an outrageouslybroad death scene.</p><p> But here's some happy news: this is the first time I've seen Seth Gilliam of The Wire onstage.</p><p> Gilliam may have stumbled a tad on the text during the performance I saw.</p><p> But he had presence onstage and Gilliam was imbued with a sense of purpose and fun.</p><p> If a villain can't enjoy the favors of two women of power while playing one off the other, what can he enjoy? I look forward to seeing Gilliam again.</p><p> As for Waterston, he surely knows that sometimes a mountaineer climbs the same peak several times before they find the path to the top that's the most satisfying, whether because it's the swiftest or hardestor fulfills some inner sense of the "right" way to climb it.</p><p> And sometimes they get to the top only todiscover it's a cloudy day.</p><p> Only a return visit can offer the promise of sun and a glorious view.</p><p> The Theater Season 2011-2012 (on a four star scale)The Agony And The Ecstasy Of Steve Jobs ** 1/2 All-American ** All's Well That Ends Well/Shakespeare in the Park ** The Atmosphere Of Memory 1/2 * Broadway By The Year: 1997 ** 1/2 Chinglish * 1/2 Crane Story ** Cymbeline at Barrow Street Theatre *** Follies *** 1/2 MOST POPULAR ON HUFFPOST 1 of 2 Fox News Viewers Know Less Than People Who Watch No News: Study Loyal Dog Refuses To Leave Owner's Grave, Goes Week Without Food Charlie Angus What if They Declared an Emergency and No One Came?
