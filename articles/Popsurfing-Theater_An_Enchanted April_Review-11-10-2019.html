SUNDAY, NOVEMBER 10, 2019 THEATER: "Enchanted April" -- The Charming Tale That Cannot Fail (Much) AN ENCHANTED APRIL ** 1/2 out of **** THEATRE ROW It's 1922 and four women -- four strangers, really -- impulsively find themselves renting the castle San Salvatore on the coast of theMediterranean for the month of April.</p><p> It's absurd.</p><p> A castle? For theentire month of April? Sunshine? And wisteria? They all have their reasons.</p><p> Lotty is a timid soul but when she spots an ad offering a castle for rent she somehow gins up the nerve toapproach another woman in her club.</p><p> They've never spoken despiteattending the same church on Sundays.</p><p> Lotty plants the idea and her friend Rose Arbuthnot tries to resist but then suggests maybe simply writing for the particulars wouldn't be so wrong.</p><p> Before you know it,they're advertising for roomies to share the expense and up pop thedour Mrs.</p><p> Fisher and the glamorous Lady Caroline Dester.</p><p> They alldecamp to Italy and the romantic setting conjures up the usual magic.</p><p> The women blossom, become friends, romances are begun or rekindled with loved ones and it truly is enchanted.</p><p> That's the essential plot of The Enchanted April by Elizabeth von Arnim.</p><p> It was published in 1922, between the wars and is the sort of light comic novel the British do better than anyone else.</p><p> Heck, as far as I can tell, no one else even tries to write these sorts of books.</p><p> It'ssweet, perceptive, very funny in the kindest and most charming sort ofway and well why not say it...enchanting.</p><p> One hundred years havecome and gone and I can recommend it unreservedly.</p><p> It's been turned into a play several times, a radio play, a few musicals and two feature films.</p><p> The most recent movie is perhaps the mostpopular adaptation of all.</p><p> That 1991 film was nominated for an Oscarand tweaked the story ever so slightly, as most adaptations can and must.</p><p> Joan Plowright was especially good as the starchy Mrs.</p><p> FisherMICHAEL GILTZ AT WORK Michael Giltz is a freelance writer based in NYC and can be reached atmgiltz@pipeline.com FAVORITE LINKS Americablog Five O'Clock Lightning baseball blogDeep Pop -- Lori Lakin's Blog The Back Page -- Jason Page on ESPN Radio Cine-Blog -- George Robinson's Blog Documents On Art & Cinema - Try as she might to be dismissive or rude, everyone is charmed by her every utterance.</p><p> Here, she ishaughty, which is fine.</p><p> She's also haunted by the recent horrors of thewar, where Lady good.</p><p> Four women come to Italy, clash a little, loosen up and take a plunge intonewer and better lives.</p><p> The big problem is the score.</p><p> C.</p><p> Michael Perry did the music and he and Hansen tackle the lyrics.</p><p> It's all recitative tunes, with aimless, meandering melodic lines, the sort of thing people think of as a laStephen Sondheim though of course Sondheim always offers greatmelodies.</p><p> You rarely get anything approaching a proper song, justvocalizing about the lovely view or this and that from a modestly voiced cast.</p><p> It wouldn't matter that most of the performers are not belters if they had some charming tunes to deliver.</p><p> But alas, theflowers bloom but the songs do not.</p><p> This being a musical, that is afatal flaw.</p><p> The story
