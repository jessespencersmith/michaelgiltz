Would she get along with the other judges? As Ellen herself says, she may not be a music industry professional but she certainlyknows a thing or two about entertainment.</p><p> To me, she's the stand-in for the fan who watches at home and thinksthey could pick a winner too.</p><p> Though in interviews Ellen suggested she found Simon a bit abrasive (to the contestants), none of that was visible tonight.</p><p> She joined the panel very smoothly, offering up criticisms andcomments with the wry humor that comes so easily to her.</p><p> We'll have to wait for some fireworks or head-butting tosee how she does down the road.</p><p> But so far, Ellen was a perfect fit.</p><p> Otherwise, we watched singers step up in groups of eight and perform a number.</p><p> I won't say I took pleasure in seeing people go home when I questioned them getting a ticket to Hollywood in the first place.</p><p> That would be mean.</p><p> But having my initial impression reinforced by seeing them sent packing certainly made me say, "See, I knew it." Here he sang "I Don't Need No Doctor," a modest hit for Ray Charles and covered recently by John Mayer (and co-written by Ashford and Simpson; who knew?).</p><p> Casey played someserious blues guitar and was a lot more winning than I expected.</p><p> He definitely made the most of his Hollywood shot.</p><p> MARY POWERS -- This 28-year-old mom looked too much like a Pat Benatar wannabe in her audition (and copycatted Benatar in her vocals too much) for my taste.</p><p> Well, there's something to be said for maturity.</p><p> Unlike so many of the kids (who looked like deers staring at headlights), she came on stage with quiet confidence and beltedout Pink's "Sober" with aplomb.</p><p> Still needs work on her look but she won me over.</p><p> CRYSTAL BOWERSOX -- Another audition that went by so quickly I wasn't sure what to think (though I did enjoy her laid-back spin on "Piece Of My Heart").</p><p> Here she strummed acoustic guitar and sang Carole King's "(You Make Me Feel Like) A Natural Woman" with such winning charm that the kids in the audience spontaneously sang backup.</p><p> Very, very winning.</p><p> The rest who got through were either new to me or reconfirmed the impression they made in their auditions.</p><p> KATIE STEVENS -- Very confident, no frills rendition of Stevie Wonder's "For Once In My Life." ANDREW GARCIA -- A very cool, Raul Midon-like spin on Paula Abdul's "Straight Up." We'll be hearing this arrangement again, trust me.</p><p> Playing an instrument is definitely a help, if you're good.</p><p> Two skills are always more impressive than one.</p><p> JANELL WHEELER -- Sang Estelle's "American Boy," also accompanying herself on guitar.</p><p> Very good.</p><p> HAELEY VAUGHN -- This was a bit iffy for me.</p><p> She played the guitar okay and was quite sharp on her vocals.</p><p> Very rough performance and yet I still kept thinking she was pretty darn cute and winning. (The giggle at the end helped.) Torn between putting her through another day on personality.</p><p> Cute but not great.</p><p> The judges probably felt the samething but she'll have to get over her nerves and deliver or this is as far as she'll go.</p><p> LILLY SCOTT -- Sang "Lullaby Of Birdland" and played the guitar.</p><p> Very Madeleine Peyroux and quite charming. (Lose the earrings, though.) Pegged the tune as by Ella Fitzgerald, which of course prompted me to look up authorship.</p><p> I didn't know the music was by George Shearing! (Lyrics by George David Weiss, who also co-wrote "What A WonderfulWorld") Blossom Dearie had the biggest chart hit with it.</p><p> And Lily did great by the tune.</p><p> She's a comer.</p><p> MICHAEL LYNCHE -- The personal trainer, Michael performed while his wife was potentially in labor with their first child.</p><p> One assumes and hopes the birth went as smoothly, for Michael sailed through with a rendition of John Mayer's "Waiting On The World To Change."
