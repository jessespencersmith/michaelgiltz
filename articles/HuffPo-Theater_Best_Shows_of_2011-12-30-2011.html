This year I have.</p><p> It's a rare privilege and I know it.</p><p> First, most people simply couldn't afford to go to that many shows even if they wanted to.</p><p> Second unless you live in New York City, your options are severely limited in the US, with the rare exception of theater towns like Chicago.</p><p> Great live theater can (and should) be seen all over the country but New York City and London are still meccas.</p><p> Third, I get to see the shows in ideal seats.</p><p> Not since I was on staff (briefly)at New York magazine in the 1990s have I had this much access to theater.</p><p> So believe me when I say I know how lucky I am.</p><p> Thanks to all the publicity firms and shows thataccommodated me this year.</p><p> Thanks to all the artists who put in the hard work to create the shows.</p><p> It should be clear from my reviews that I like championing a good show much more than ripping apart a bad one.</p><p> When something doesn't work, it's agony for actors to have to go onstage and face that factnight after night. (I only have to face it once.) Even a spectacle like Spiderman: Turn Off The Dark that clearly has its eyes on being a blockbuster is a labor of love for all involved that means years of hard work.So my negative reviews try to be constructive.</p><p> I'm no show doctor but if I can encourage the elements that do work and see some way towards making a show better, why not speak up? THE BEST THEATER OF 2011 1.</p><p> Sons Of The Prophet -- the best play of the year with the best cast.</p><p> Stephen Karam is the real deal.</p><p> I paid to see it when money was tight and it was the happiest purchase I made this year. 2.</p><p> Once -- a show that seemed a rather iffy idea delivers beautifully; here's hoping they make it even better when transferring it to Broadway 3.</p><p> Sleep No More -- Punchdrunk's work is always gripping but this immersive extravaganza spin on Macbeth is the most welcome hit of the year 4.</p><p> The Interminable Suicide Of Gregory Church -- Daniel Kitson's one-man show was my first chance to experience his considerable skill.</p><p> He returns to St.</p><p> Ann's Warehouse in January in a new show.</p><p> Buy your tickets immediately. 5.</p><p> PigPen Theatre Co.'s The Mountain Song and Nightmare Story -- these guys formed their troupe at Carnegie Mellon.</p><p> Their shows combine some of my favorite elements -- folk tales, Americanamusic, puppetry -- into moving, whimsical and charming works that show them bursting with talent andideas.</p><p> One played at FringeFest in Manhattan and the other in Brooklyn and I would have gladly gone tothe other three boroughs to see them three more times.</p><p> Can't wait to see what they do next.</p><p> Word is they'll be coming to Off Broadway in 2012 with a new show. 6.</p><p> All's Well That Ends Well at the Globe in London -- the Globe is one of my favorite theatrical haunts in the world, thanks to rock bottom prices (five pounds if you can stand in the pit, which happento be the best seats) and great quality.</p><p> But this peerless production directed by John Dove and with amatchless cast headed by Ellie Percy was a marvel from top to bottom.7.</p><p> The School For Lies -- David Ives had a banner year, but I'd argue this brilliant adaptation of Moliere's The Misanthrope at Classic Stage Company was the peak, thanks to brilliance all around, led byactors Mamie Gummer and Hamish Linklater.</p><p> Tremendous fun I wish I could see again. 8.</p><p> Peter and The Starcatcher - People went crazy over the inventive staging of Cymbeline by Theatre For A New Audience, which I enjoyed.</p><p> But I thought director Roger Rees did an even better, similar job in creating this tale about Peter Pan with the simplest and most pleasing theatrical devices.</p><p> A winner.Another show I paid to see and thrilled to have done so.</p><p> It's coming to Broadway in the spring so don't miss out. 9.</p><p> Three Sisters -- another winner for Classic Stage Company, which had a great year, to say the least.</p><p> Chekhov is exceptionally hard to get right but this production did it, led by Maggie Gyllenhaal, JulietRylance, Jessica Hecht and Peter Sarsgaard with subtle direction by Austin Pendleton. (I'm stillheartbroken I missed his musical A Minister's Wife with Bobby Steggert.) No review because I paid to see it, knowing that CSC is one of the safest bets in town.10.</p><p> The Book Of Mormon -- yes, it's very funny indeed and for me the show soars emotionally and comedically thanks to Andrew Rannells as Elder Price. 11.</p><p> How To Succeed In Business Without Really Trying -- Everyone else was gaga over AnythingHOT ON FACEBOOK 1 of 3 HOT ON TWITTER 1 of 2 HUFFPOST'S BIG NEWS PAGES Snooki Smoking Ask The Parent Coach Chris Christie Arizona Politics Natural Disasters Wisconsin Shadow Conventions 2012 Fox News MORE BIG NEWS PAGES Â» Slate Articles Â»Did You See This? It made clear that Aaron Tveit is aBroadway star triple threat (four if you count his handsome looks).</p><p> While the score is not as solid asHairspray, every other element is top-notch from the terrific book to the smashing sets, costumes,choreography and ensemble.</p><p> This is how Broadway is done.</p><p> An ideal sexy show for businessmen who don't like musicals (how often can they see Jersey Boys?), it inexplicably came and went.</p><p> Too bad. 16.</p><p> Arcadia -- This revival was also passed over by many but I love Tom Stoppard and it was a treat to see Billy Crudup tackle another role after seeing him in the original Broadway production.</p><p> A major workfrom a major playwright presented with care.17.</p><p> Follies -- what a curious show.</p><p> I think this production is the best I've ever seen and it's bursting with great talent and great songs but I still think it could be done even better and the static second act brought further to life.</p><p> Yet I'd love to see it again before it goes. 18.</p><p> War Horse -- My favorite show for bragging rights since I could compare the Broadway production to the London version I saw a year or two earlier.</p><p> Far superior to the Steven Spielberg film and indeed even the book. 19.</p><p> Hand To God -- A show about a sock puppet possessed by the devil? A fun cast led by an inspired Steven Boyer make it work.</p><p> But here's hoping they keep working on it to make a fun play that stays inyour mind even better before transferring to Off Broadway.20.</p><p> Hugh Jackman: Back On Broadway -- I was afraid not to include it.</p><p> If I didn't, Jackman might just show up at my apartment and perform the entire show in my living room until
