sonorous and lovely it makes the rest of us sound like Cockney layabouts.</p><p> LORD OF THE FLIES * 1/2 out of **** The Regents Park Open Air Theatre is akin to New York's Shakespeare in the Park, a novel way to experience theater.</p><p> New artistic director Timothy Sheader has smartly expanded the reach of the theater by programming more adventurous fare.</p><p> It doesn't always work, but the gambit will make this venue healthier in the long run.</p><p> I'm simply not a fan of Lord Of The Flies in any of its forms.</p><p> The story is so blunt and obvious as parable that the fun is taken out, though it does make it catnip for teachers who want a short book their students might actually read.</p><p> The tale depicts British schoolboys crash-landed on an island.</p><p> Decent folk wouldexpect these lads to pull together and be sensible.</p><p> Surely they'll care for the little ones and stay safe whilewaiting for rescue? Instead, the little beasties soon degenerate into savagery.</p><p> This was a revelation onceupon a time, apparently, though anyone who's ever watched little kids at play should know they can becasually cruel to one another.</p><p> This adaptation by Nigel Williams has a few good ideas, mainly adding in some evocative dance and choreography.</p><p> They should have gone further and treated the story as pure myth and ritual.</p><p> The lads --led by handsome young Alistair Toovey -- give their all, but to no avail.</p><p> It doesn't help that this story inparticular should go barreling towards its conclusion in about 80 minutes, not pause for a 20-minutebreak.</p><p> Tech credits however are superb.</p><p> You walk into the theater and say, "Wow, what an amazing set!"thanks to smoke and wreckage that makes the stage look like the opening scene of TV's Lost, complete with a plane snapped in two.</p><p> Unfortunately, you leave with the same thought, "what an amazing set." That is never a good sign, though full credit to designer Jon Bausor.</p><p> OPERATION GREENFIELD ** 1/2 out of **** Little Bulb Theatre is a small company that's proving adept at both unique theatrical offerings and gaining attention for them, both key elements to survival in fringe theater.</p><p> They are certainly one to watch.</p><p> Their latest show is Operation Greenfield at Soho Theatre.</p><p> It's a goofy little tale about four kids growing up in a small town in England who form a Christian rock group to compete in the local battle of the bands contest.</p><p> The script boasts a number of ideas, none of them fully developed.</p><p> Music as life-changingexperience has its fullest treatment when they unexpectedly dive into a David Bowie tune and have theirminds blown; it is the show's most theatrical and satisfying moment.</p><p> However, they soon retreat tocalmer musical waters.</p><p> Music as dangerous gateway to experimentation gets brief tryouts thanks to oneband member being tempted by cigarettes and two of the girls suddenly making out.</p><p> This too goes The Twitter Typo That Exposed Weiner Engadget: WWDC 2011 liveblog: Steve Jobs talks iOS 5, OS X Lion, iCloud and more!
