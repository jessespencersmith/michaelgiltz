Her memoir "West With TheNight" is rediscovered with regularity and no wonder: it's filled with stirring tales and captures the joy of flight likefew others. The fact that Markham crossed paths (to say the least) with the likes of Isak Dinesen while strikingher own fearless path in Kenya makes her all the more appealing. Still, for those who have read that classicmemoir, telling Markham's life in a work of fiction is rather audacious. Happily, Paula McLain -- author of themassive bestseller "The Paris Wife" -- is up to the task. She gets under the skin of Markham, brings herchildhood more vividly to the front and against all expectations brings us right up to the moment when ourheroine literally begins to soar. The story of a famed aviatrix that stops before the flying begins? McLain wiselyforges her own path, just like Markham, circling around and under and through that famed memoir while creatinga fully realized character.
