But I kept sort ofavoiding it, even though I didn't expect the movie to bother meemotionally beyond whatever the movie itself might achieve. But it did.And it was certainly the quietest New York audience I've been aroundat a movie theater in a long time, if ever. Not a sound from anyone frombeginning to end. Director Paul Greengrass did a terrific job -- nosurprise since his "Bloody Sunday" was an exceptional look at the Irishtroubles that felt so authentic you wondered if it was a documentaryand his "Bourne Supremacy" proved he had a way with action andviolence. It was matter of fact, moving and sad. I don't ever need to see"Mission: Impossible" again because it moved me so little.
