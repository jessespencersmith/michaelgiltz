She is quickly seconded to the Ministry’s latest endeavor: a heart-warming tale of bravery and rescue at Dunkirk. It’s allcompletely fabricated, of course, but what does that matter when thenation’s morale is at stake? This story is set in London during WW II, with a young woman leaping from the Ministry of Information and boring old patriotic shorts to an unexpected opportunity working on a feature film about Dunkirk. The government wantsmore female heroines featured in war movies to boost morale, so this movie focuses on two young women who took part in that famed naval rescue. Our heroine Catrin Cole is expected to deliver the mushy, feminine dialogue beneath the dignity of the men delivering the script. Vain actors, cynical writers, dodgy politicians and bombs falling all around? Wonderful!
