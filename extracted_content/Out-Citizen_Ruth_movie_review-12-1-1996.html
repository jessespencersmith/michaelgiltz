Citizen Ruth movie review

Published: 12/1/1996 | Publication: Out

Original PDF: Download PDF

Citizen Ruth directed by Alexander Payne A CAUSTIC YET VERY funny movie about abortion rights? You bet. Hapless, glue-sniffing, pregnant drifter Ruth (Laura Dern) finds herself pushed and pulled by pro­ and anti-choice forces when a judge offers to reduce her jail time for shoplifting if she terminates her pregnancy. In opposite corners are Swoosie Kurtz, playing an undercover lesbian pro-choice activist (pictured, page 72, with Dern), and Mary Kay Place, as a sweet but implacable pro-life housewife. Though the movie takes shots at both sides, it really gives it to the religious Right­ especially during an outrageous cameo by Burt Reynolds as a holier-than-thou pro-life leader. Still, you'll root for the none-roo-bright Ruth-who can never think further than her next chance to abuse a substance-to outwit them all. Dern deserves another Oscar nominati on for her hilarious, edgy performance, and the clever screenplay by director Alexander Payne and Jim Taylor builds wonderfully . Comparisons to Preston Sturges are not out of order. (Miramax)-MIcHAEL GILTZ DEC/JAN 1997 OUT \
