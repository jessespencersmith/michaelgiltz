The et . assersteln Playwright and best friend Paul Rudnick tells why he wouldn't miss the revival of Wendy Wasserstein's Uncommon Women and Oth­ ers opening October 26. "I've known Wendy since I was an undergradu­ ate at Yale. I did the props and Wendy played a French maid in a Feydeau farce. Even though she's gone on to win the Pulitzer, I must say, she played the maid astonishingly well, especially for a woman who I don't believe has ever cleaned a hotel room. "Wendy had a rough time at Yale. Back then, the vogue was for imitation Sam Shepard plays­ rugged male Western plays that were usually writ­ ten by some Jewish boy from Long Island. Wendy was treated as some oddball whose work would never last."
