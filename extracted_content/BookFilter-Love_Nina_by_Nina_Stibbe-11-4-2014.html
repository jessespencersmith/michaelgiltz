What We Say Love, Nina by Nina Stibbe is a charming memoir presented as a series of letters between Stibbe and her sister. Stibbe worked as a nanny for the female head of the London Review of Books in the 1980s, allowing her to weigh in on everyonefrom her boss’s ex-husband, film director Stephen Frears, to various and sundry figures, from a decidedly humorous and domestic perspective. It’s a delight, mostly centered on family. But the real catnip for theater lovers is next-door neighbor Alan Bennett, who Stibbe first thinks might be an actor on the telly but finally susses out is a playwright.
