Those fa­ miliar with Nair's feature debut, Salaam Bombay!, a painfully real­ istic look at street children, will be especially surprised by the fan­ tastic touches of Perez, such as when Molina's breaking heart is symbolized by blood oozing out of his chest. "This one, I felt, was giving itself over to magic," says Nair, "not know­ ing where dream ends and reality begins. Cubans are definitely given to that condition. They're a passionate culture." Nair is equally passionate about plunging ahead with a career happily delayed by the birth of her son after 1991's Mis­ sissippi Masala.
