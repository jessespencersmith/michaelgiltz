That haunting score by David Raskin probably helped. Lots of extras on this classic 1944 film noir, including two commentary tracks, two A&E Biography specials (on Tierney and Vincent Price) and more. And don't forget Clifton Webb's career peak as the acerbic Waldo Lydecker. Other noirs just out: 1948's "Call Northside 777" and 1950's "Panic in the Streets" (Fox, $14.98 each). Easter Parade *** Warner Bros., $26.99 IF balding Fred Astaire (nearly 50) wasn't almost twice as old as his co-star Judy Garland (just 26), this Irving Berlin romantic musical from 1948 might have been a real classic instead of hard-to­ swallow. Still, they're troupers and you can't beat 17 Irving Berlin tunes. Sterling extras include an excellent American Masters documentary "Judy Garland: By Myself." Other musicals just out: 1953's "The Band Wagon: Special Edition" (Warner Bros., $26.99) and 1954's "Brigadoon" (Warner Bros., $19.97). Stand By Me: Deluxe Edition ***'/2 Columbia, $24.96 THE latest DVD gimmick? Including a bonus CD with the soundtrack. "Stand By Me" (1986) comes with a disc including "Lollipop," and the title tune by Ben E. King, which re­ entered the charts, thanks to this sleeper hit. The great cast includes Kiefer Sutherland. In a twist, 2000's "Miss Congeniality: Deluxe Edition Gift Set" (Warner Bros., $39.92) comes with the soundtrack to the sequel and a free movie pass to see it. Hogan's ·Heroes: First Season **,!, Paramount , $38.99 A sitcom about life in a German prisoner of war camp during World War II? Surely "Hogan's Heroes" remains one of the unlikeliest hits in TV history -especially considering its 1965 premiere was only 20 years removed from the end of the war. Silly but harmless. End of the Century: The Story of The Ramones *** Rhino, $19.99 DEE Dee's manic count-off for every tune "Onetwothreefour!" -would have been a more fun title for this look at the legendary New York band. But "End ofthe Century" captures the bittersweet feel of this fine documentary about a group that influenced everyone from the Clash to Green Day.
