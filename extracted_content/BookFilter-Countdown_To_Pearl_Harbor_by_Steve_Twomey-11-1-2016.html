What We Say A solid, straight-forward account of the twelve days leading up to the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor. That may be more of an accomplishment than I appreciate, since Pearl Harbor is an event ripe for conspiracy theory and exaggeration. Ihaven't read widely on it and this popular history seems quite reasonable. Pulitzer Prize winner Steve Twomey combs through the available record and brings to life the many disparate personalities, hidebound traditions, racism and simple human error that led to the US being caught flat-footed on December 7, 1941.
