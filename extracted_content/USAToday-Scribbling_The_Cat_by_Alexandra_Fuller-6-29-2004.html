She was born in England but spent most of her young life in war-torn Zimbabwe (then called Rhodesia) and later Malawi and Zambia. The troubled continent has been the source of her literary inspiration. Fuller's best-selling memoir, Don't Let's Go to the Dogs Tonight, vividly depicted her childhood after her father went off to war.
